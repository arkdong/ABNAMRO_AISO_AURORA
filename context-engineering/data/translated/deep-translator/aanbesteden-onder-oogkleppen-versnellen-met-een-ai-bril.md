# Procurement without blinders: accelerate with AI glasses

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

11/06/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Leontien de Waal](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/leontien-de-waal/index.html)

The Van Brienenoord Bridge squeaks and creaks. Not only under the weight of 235,000 vehicles per day, but also under pressure from a previously canceled tender. Rijkswaterstaat's cost estimate turned out to be too low, while the sole tenderer, out of caution and realism, charged significant risk surcharges. The result: a high price, no award, a lot of frustration and lost time.

## Lessons learned

The tender failed due to an overly optimistic design, an overly rigid contract with risks that are difficult for companies to mitigate and control, and a difficult dialogue between the client and the market. Fortunately, Rijkswaterstaat and other authorities now seem to approach the tenders for these types of complex projects differently: from now on, a longer preparation will be used, including additional studies, refinement of the design and a joint implementation strategy.

## Technology helping with risk inventory

Yet the question remains: why do we use so little technology in the tender phase? With AI we can analyze historical tender and realization data to make more accurate estimates. Machine learning helps recognize recurring risks and how they work out financially. For example, an AI analysis could have indicated that the combination of the UAV-GC contract types would deter market parties from the strict verification and validation requirements for the design and the delivered structure respectively.

## More objective considerations

AI can also help with the assessment of registrations. Instead of relying on the best texts and the best tender writers, we can use algorithms and prompts to assess tenders based on measurable criteria, such as previous performance, risk management and sustainability. This reduces the influence of subjective assessments and promotes a fairer selection process.

> “At the same time, we should not be naive: AI is not a magic wand.”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction

## Smart technology and craftsmanship

At the same time, we should not be naive: AI is not a magic wand. If we rely on it too much, we run the risk of what is called 'skill decay'. AI is not a replacement for experience and insight, but an enhancement of it. AI can explore solutions, but professionals must remain involved in interpretation and decision-making.

The bridge of the future requires a smarter approach. AI can help bring projects to market faster and better. This is desperately needed, because obstacles such as nitrogen and grid congestion still have to be overcome before implementation. Meanwhile, our infrastructure is aging, putting accessibility and economic growth at risk.
