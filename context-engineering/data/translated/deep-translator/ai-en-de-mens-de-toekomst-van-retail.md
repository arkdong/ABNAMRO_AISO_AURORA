# AI and humans: the future of retail

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Retail](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/retail/index.html)

24/09/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[Henk Hofstede](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/henk-hofstede/index.html)

The recent NRF 2025 retail fair in Paris focused on one theme: artificial intelligence (AI). AI is not only a means to increase efficiency, but also an essential tool to improve the shopping experience and make customer communication more personal. At the same time, personal input remains an indispensable link in strengthening the bond with customers.

The topic of AI was omnipresent at the NRF 2025 – Retail's Big Show Europe – which took place in the French capital from September 16 to 18. The buzzword popped up in almost every presentation and countless exhibitors presented specific AI solutions. Consultant [Bain & Company](https://www.bain.com/insights/the-store-is-striking-back-as-a-tech-enabled-space-driving-efficiency-experience-and-monetization/) presented a recent survey conducted among 66 CEOs of the 250 largest retailers. Three-quarters of them expect to invest significantly in store technologies over the next two years. The top three technological investments on the shop floor include improving product availability, offering more competitive prices and promotions, and strengthening customer engagement and loyalty.

## AI leads to improved results

To achieve the above goals, retailers are focusing on technologies such as AI-driven customer insights, smart shelf and inventory sensors and electronic shelf labels. According to many of the CEOs, these innovations lead to an increase in returns of 1.5 percent. In a sector where margins are under pressure, stores are quickly becoming obsolete in the eyes of consumers and competition is fierce, this is a significant improvement.

However, AI not only plays a role in efficiency, but also in personalization. Based on geolocation, AI technology can send relevant personalized offers to the mobile phones of customers near a store or on the shop floor – fully tailored to their preferences or recent searches. Customers must give permission for this due to the GDPR privacy law. This refined personalization leads to more visitors, higher conversion and more loyal customers. AI therefore adds tangible value to the shopping experience.

## Training employees is crucial

Google Cloud's Director of Retail EMEA sounded the same note on the NRF stage, highlighting how AI and AI agents are turning traditional marketing on its head. Stores and web shops are becoming increasingly data-driven, with commerce working more from personalization on the shop floor. AI is also strong in making forecasts, which results in more optimal inventory management and more efficient logistics. However, it is crucial that employees are trained to work and experiment with AI applications. Retailers who fail to do this will lose out to their competitors.

## Not everything is AI

In addition to the fair, there was also time to explore Paris. In charming neighborhoods such as Le Marais, I visited unique stores such as Acne Studios, Jimmy Fairly 3000 and The Broken Arm that sell fashion, design and lifestyle products in minimalist, innovative and creatively designed stores. Here it is mainly about the store itself, the range, the atmosphere and the personal attention. AI hardly plays a role here. Iconic department stores such as Printemps Haussmann, Galeries Lafayette Haussmann (with the spectacular dome from 1912 and a breathtaking roof terrace) and the more modern Galleries Lafayette Champs-Élysées also show how luxury, human service and shopping experience go hand in hand. There is a remarkable amount of staff present here. Lancôme's flagship store on the Champs-Élysées is an example of how personal attention to the customer leads to a unique brand experience.

## Retail is people business

My conclusion after this trip: in-store AI technology will become indispensable and standard – both to save costs and to serve customers on a more personal level. Yet the human factor remains crucial. The future of retail lies in the synergy between technology and human input. Stores are not only transaction-driven, but also intended to strengthen brand value and strengthen the bond with consumers. AI offers speed and sophistication, but it's the people in the stores who make the real difference. Or as the CEO of Sephora, the popular international beauty and cosmetics chain, succinctly said on the NRF stage: “Retail is people business.” Ultimately, it is the employees who connect with the customer and bring the brand experience to life.
