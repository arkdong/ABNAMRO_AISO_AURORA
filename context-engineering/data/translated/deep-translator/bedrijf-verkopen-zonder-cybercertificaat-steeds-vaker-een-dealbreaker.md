# Selling a company without a cyber certificate is increasingly a deal breaker

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cybersecurity](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/veilig/index.html)

18/08/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

Mergers and acquisitions are given an extra test in due diligence: cybersecurity. Buyers and investors are increasingly demanding demonstrable digital resilience – and this has everything to do with risks that would otherwise only become visible after an acquisition.

## Why cybersecurity has become decisive

Buyers do not want a digital time bomb: systems must be safe, incidents must be handled properly, chain risks must be controlled. Companies without certification or with vulnerabilities in their supplier network are at risk of:

- the deal is canceled;
- the value is adjusted;
- or substantial guarantees are required.

## NIS2 as new standard

The European NIS2 directive sets requirements for thousands of companies and their chain. During acquisitions, buyers are increasingly looking to see whether the target company meets these requirements – or whether there will be a compliance issue.

## NIS2 Quality Mark as supporting document

For those looking to prepare for sales or financing, the [NIS2 Quality Mark (QM)](https://nis2qualitymark.eu/) offers a scalable, practical alternative to more stringent standards. This way you as an organization demonstrate that you manage risks - also in the chain.

## Strategic forward thinking pays off

Digital security is not only a compliance issue, but also a strategic choice. Anyone who has their digital hygiene in order will retain their value.
