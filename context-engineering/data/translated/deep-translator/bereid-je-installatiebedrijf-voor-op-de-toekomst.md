# Prepare your installation company for the future

The prospects for the installation sector are mixed and require strategic choices. During the recent Techniek Nederland webinar, the economic prospects for the installation sector from 2026 were discussed. Overall, the industry will grow slightly in the coming years, with a strong foundation in residential construction.

Both new residential construction and sustainability offer good prospects for growth, while non-residential construction and infrastructure are developing more moderately. Important drivers for installers are the energy transition, the increasing installation rate in buildings and the role that ICT plays. These developments are further explained in a recently published [report](https://www.technieknederland.nl/home/leadgen/download-economie-vooruitzichten-2026-en-verder/).

## Opportunities in a changing market

The prospects for the industry require adaptability from installation companies: investing in technological solutions, digitalization & cyber security and cooperation within the chain will become important conditions for remaining competitive and relevant. Innovative energy saving concepts, smart installations and maintenance solutions play a key role in this. Entrepreneurs would do well to examine their strategy. Especially in a changing market, there are opportunities for companies that adapt in time.

## Installation materials vulnerable to price increases

Meanwhile, the sector is struggling with structural challenges such as labor shortages, an aging population and dependencies on international supplies. Consider, for example, installation materials such as heat pumps, inverters and electrical components that are imported. These supplies are vulnerable to disruptions in global trade and geopolitics, which could lead to shortages or price increases.

## Solutions in a tight labor market

During the webinar, Leontien de Waal, Construction sector banker at ABN AMRO, discussed the implications of the developments for entrepreneurs. The structural challenge of staff shortages was also discussed. Investing in attractive employment practices, training and responding to changing expectations of Gen Zers is important for future earning capacity. In addition, the use of AI plays an important role in relieving the burden on employees. There are more and more useful AI tools on both the work preparation and execution side, allowing you to work faster and more efficiently.

The Dutch construction sector has been one of the best performing sectors in recent years, despite several challenges facing the sector. Examples are the nitrogen dossier, the significant price increases for energy and building materials and also the personnel shortages. Driven by trends, innovations and legislation and regulations, the relevance of themes such as sustainability and environmental impact is growing and the sector is looking for a new balance.
