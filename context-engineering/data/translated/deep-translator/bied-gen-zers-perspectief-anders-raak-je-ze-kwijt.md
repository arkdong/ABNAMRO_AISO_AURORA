# Modderkolk: Offer Gen Zers perspective, otherwise you will lose them

[Entrepreneurial Stories](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/ondernemershistories/index.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

03/06/2025 • 5 minutes reading

[Leontien de Waal](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/leontien-de-waal/index.html)

The industry is indispensable to our economy. There are plenty of opportunities within the industry, but it is important to continue to innovate and appeal to young generations of technical talent. Modderkolk from Wijchen (Gelderland) has been succeeding in this with flying colors since 1921.

What does that mean for the specialist in industrial automation and installation technology? A modern image and a good influx of Generation Z - of the 250 employees, more than a quarter were born after 1996. Owner Simon de Luij explains how he continually prepares the organization for the market conditions and employees of tomorrow.

## A look at Generation Z with practical training

This article is part of a study by ABN AMRO into the youngest generation in the labor market: Generation Z, born between 1997 and 2012. In our research we specifically focus on those with a practical education. What do they find important in their work and what are they looking for in an employer? The message that emerges from our report is clear. Practically educated Gen Zers especially want:

1. Clarity about salary and contract form
2. Knowing what concrete work they will do and how they can grow
3. That the basics are in order: good equipment and professional guidance

Curious about our full analysis? Read the complete report here '[What practically educated Gen Zers really want](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/wat-praktisch-opgeleide-gen-zers-echt-willen.html)'

## Ready for the future, for over a century

“[Modderkolk](https://www.modderkolk.nl/) was founded by my grandfather in 1921. My parents took over at some point, and I have now been at the helm for about forty years,” Simon begins. To remain successful for so long, it is very important to completely change course periodically. 'We did that in 2007, for example. I then set four clear goals: continue to flourish forever, put customers first, fully commit to the development of our people and ensure a high level of motivation. We are still reaping the benefits of that change, even when it comes to attracting and retaining new talent.'

## Learning and development as the key to success

2012 was a crucial year on the way to the development goal. Then the company – together with the Koning Willem I College in Den Bosch – set up its own education department. Under the name Modderkolk Academy, they bundle all training, education and courses - including a business school. 'What we mainly wanted to achieve with our Academy was to attract and retain more people in technology - for our company, but also out of love for the profession.' According to Simon, finding people is not that difficult, nor is finding suitable work for them. Keeping them on board is the challenge.

'We do this by providing them with intensive and personal guidance. We currently have 27 students, who all have their own mentor here. Finding enough good supervisors can be difficult, but it is essential: without them, a student quickly disappears, often completely from the industry. Our master-apprentice model is very popular: young people simply like to learn under the wings of an experienced colleague.'

> “Finding people is not that difficult, nor is finding suitable work for them. Keeping them on board is the challenge”

Simon de Luij

## Commitment to the cause

To boost motivation, Simon focused on three sub-goals. 'Firstly, the purpose of the organization must be aligned with that of our people. Secondly, people should be able to organize their work themselves as much as possible. And finally, mastery is very important: they must be able to become the best at something. If you can offer them that perspective, there is a good chance that your people will feel involved in and committed to the company. This also applies to the youngest generation, according to Simon. 'They want to contribute to a greater goal and perspective on a good growth path. Can you give them that certainty for a longer period of time? Then you significantly reduce the chance that they will become bored.'

## Quickly into the depths

'We quickly put young professionals to work. They like to learn by doing and appreciate a working environment in which they can develop from day one,” says Simon. 'For MBO students, this often means more intensive guidance, because they enter at a young age and largely learn their profession in practice. HBO graduates bring a broader theoretical basis, but often still need practical experience to apply their knowledge. This requires customization, where we respond to the development needs of every employee.'

'In addition, the guidance differs per field. Technicians mainly learn through experience and guidance on the work floor, while IT professionals, for example, more often work independently. By giving them confidence and space, they grow in their role. What they have in common is that they are less into direct contact than older generations: they prefer to communicate with colleagues digitally. But they do show more sense of community and entrepreneurship than, for example, Generation Y. And both attach great importance to their work-life balance.'

> “Students immediately become a full part of the team – including salary and autonomy”

Simon de Luij

Owner Modderkolk

## Generation Z demands customization

Knowing what the youngest generation needs is one thing. It is much more important for an employer to respond adequately to this. 'With us, students immediately become a full part of the team - including salary and autonomy. Because they find their work-life balance important, they can work flexibly and we schedule them close to home where possible. Moreover, they can determine their desired specialization very efficiently during speed dates with project leaders.' But Simon also caters to community-minded Gen Zers on a social level: 'We have built a company restaurant and organize many fun activities, at company and team level. From drinks to boot camps - a number of employees even participated together in the Strong Viking Obstacle Run.'

## The formula for success: keep changing

'That total package makes us an attractive employer. What is regularly confirmed: people who leave us often come back to us. Between 25 and 30 percent of our employees are from Generation Z – at 37 years old, our average age is much lower than the rest of the industry. Each and every one of them is a great performance. But that doesn't mean we can rest on our laurels. To appeal to the professionals of tomorrow, we must invest today. In our business school, culture and way of working. This way we can continue to offer Gen Zers and subsequent generations tailor-made solutions.'

## Want to captivate, attract and retain practically trained Gen Zers? These are Modderkolk's tips

- Offer beautiful and visible craftsmanship with clear social relevance.
- Invest in personal guidance by an experienced mentor.
- Have students participate as quickly as possible. The longer they have to wait for contributions and earnings, the greater the risk that their patience will run out.
- Provide an attractive growth perspective: make it clear what steps they can take.
- Pay attention to work-life balance, for example with flexible schedules or shorter travel times.
- Organize social activities to stimulate bonding within teams.
