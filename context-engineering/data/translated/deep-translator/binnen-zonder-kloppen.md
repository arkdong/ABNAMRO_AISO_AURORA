# Enter without knocking

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Transport & Logistics](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/logistiek/index.html)

[Bart Banning](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/bart-banning/index.html)

The digital transformation has fundamentally changed the logistics sector. But cyber criminals are very interested in the data that is exchanged within valuable logistics chains. A successful cyber attack is painful for your company, employees and your reputation with clients.

> “An attack on a transport company can disrupt the entire chain.”

Bart Banning

Sector Banker Transport & Logistics

The digital transformation is happening rapidly and has fundamentally changed the logistics sector. Data systems and automation are no longer just supportive, but direct logistics operations. They lead to higher efficiency and more insight through real-time data exchange within chains. However, the transition to the digital organization also has a dark side. Cyber ​​criminals are very interested in the data that is exchanged within valuable logistics chains. A successful cyber attack feels like an acute robbery with consequences for continuity, returns and reputation. Digitization a strategic theme? Certainly! But so is cyber resilience.

## Digitization continues

The [The Logistics Trend Map 2024](https://www.maersk.com/insights/integrated-logistics/2025/03/27/logistics-trend-map-exploring-global-logistics-trends-2025) of the Danish carrier Maersk is clear. Digital transformation is one of the defining trends for logistics companies. Predictive analytics leads to better risk management and more accurate decision-making. This has been proven during the COVID-19 pandemic. Digital systems then enabled organizations to monitor bottlenecks and quickly adjust activities. In addition, automated warehouses and robotics increase productivity and are a solution to the structural labor market shortage. According to industry club [Dilas and real estate advisor Savills](https://www.savills.nl/research_articles/251853/363003-0), 83 percent of Dutch logistics companies will invest in digitalization in 2025 (2024: 60 percent). The size of these investments is expected to grow globally by 9.8 percent per year until 2030, with artificial intelligence as a strong driver.

## Cyber security requires attention

Rapid developments in digitalization also require investments in cybersecurity. Recent research by EyeSecurity shows that one in five companies in the Netherlands are at risk of becoming the victim of a cyber attack with a major impact. Shippers' organization Evofenedex recently warned about criminals who abuse reliable carriers for [cargo theft trick](https://www.evofenedex.nl/actualheden/risk-op-laden theft-door-nieuwe-truc-van-criminalelen#:~:text=28%2D04%2D2025%20Criminelen%20gaan,het%20transporten%20van%20highquality%20products.) via online freight exchanges. And new cyber research from ABN AMRO shows that 80 percent of transport entrepreneurs indicate that cybercrime is a risk within their organization; considerably higher than the average across all sectors. And 16 percent of transport entrepreneurs indicate that they have suffered financial damage due to cyber incidents. Recent cyber events from [TVM](https://www.tvm.nl/prevention/cybersecurity/cyberonderzoek/clients-vertellen-over-cyberattack) and the Schuttevaer Expo teach us that many companies know that they need to become more resilient to cyber attacks, and also want to invest, but are looking for how to do so. There is therefore still a lot of work to be done to increase resilience.

## Discipline of own employees

Implementation of cyber security measures is essential business hygiene in 2025. It not only requires investments in systems, but also attention to staff behavior. According to the ABN AMRO report, the biggest challenge for cyber security lies in the discipline of its own employees. The logistics sector scores on discipline at 53 percent, significantly higher than the average of 37 percent across all sectors. Phishing emails are most effective in the logistics sector. According to consultant [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/sustainable-inclusive-growth/charts/phishing-with-ai-is-cybersecuritys-new-hook), the number of [phishing](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/wat-is-phishing.html) attacks has increased explosively across the board since the arrival of ChatGPT. 138 percent. IBM adds that the average response time in 2024 across all sectors was 72 days. A nightmare for logistics entrepreneurs, who prefer to think in hours.

## NIS2 is there for a reason

The speed at which new cyber threats emerge and the ability to continue to comply with legislation are the most important reasons for companies to invest more in cyber security in 2025. The [NIS2 legislation](https://doorpassen.abnamro.nl/producten-en-diensten/veilig-ondernemen/nis2/), which is expected to come into effect in the Netherlands in the third quarter of 2025, clearly indicates where attention should be focused to prevent cyber attacks.

One thing is certain: the group of hackers is getting bigger, bolder and smarter every day. And artificial intelligence is also used by them. On the other hand, logistics is not possible without further development of the digital infrastructure. So there is only one option: invest in its security. And if things do go wrong, make sure you have backup and a plan to take immediate action. That saves time and costs. After all, hackers always enter without knocking.
