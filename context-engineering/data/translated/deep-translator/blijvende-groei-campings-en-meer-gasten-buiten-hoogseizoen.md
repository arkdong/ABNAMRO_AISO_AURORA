The number of overnight stays at campsites fell last year for the first time in years. Despite this 7 percent decline, the number of overnight stays is still higher than in 2019, and the number of overnight stays by foreign guests remained virtually stable. This suggests that Dutch campsites are able to maintain the growth they have seen since 2020.

Between 2014 and 2024, the number of overnight stays by foreign guests grew by 90 percent to 26 million overnight stays. Within this, foreigners now account for 30 percent of overnight stays at Dutch campsites. German visitors make up the largest share, accounting for 25 percent of all overnight stays. The growth in overnight stays by visitors from countries other than the traditionally important Germany is striking. For example, the number of visitors from Switzerland grew by 189 percent between 2014 and 2024 to 107 thousand overnight stays. The number of overnight stays by Swedes and Spaniards grew by 60 and 53 percent respectively to 16 and 29 thousand overnight stays. In absolute numbers, this growth does not result in many extra overnight stays, but the Dutch campsite seems to be becoming more attractive to tourists from other countries than before.

## Length of camping season is increasing

The camping season seems to be getting longer and longer. For example, stays have increased slightly in popularity in the first and fourth quarters. The share of overnight stays in the months April to September fell from 95 percent to 91 percent between 2012 and 2024. Popularity also increased in the period just outside the high season, as the share of overnight stays from May to August fell from 80 percent to 74 percent.

The causes of this can be attributed to climate change, a higher number of campers that are less sensitive to weather influences, an aging population that is less tied to school holidays and relaxation of rules by municipalities to keep campsites open longer. Moreover, the growing supply of luxury tents with all kinds of extra facilities such as a bed, a refrigerator and a stove makes camping more attractive for a larger part of the year. These are also important reasons that the number of overnight stays has grown since 2019.

## Expectations for campsites are rosy

In ABN AMRO's subsector forecast for 2025, we expect the number of overnight stays at campsites to grow by 3 percent this year, despite last year's decline. The weather remains an important factor; Last year, a rainy spring in particular led to a lower number of overnight stays.

The high inflation of the past period may lead holidaymakers to look for cheaper alternatives. The campsites are well positioned for this, because they are relatively cheaper compared to other types of accommodation accommodation. The NBTC's Holiday Sentiment Monitor (January 2025) shows that 40 percent of Dutch people and 52 percent of Germans are adjusting their holidays because of the increased prices. Within this group, 45 percent of Dutch people and 42 percent of Germans opt for a cheaper stay.

The increasing geopolitical unrest may lead to both Dutch and foreign holidaymakers who have yet to book choosing a destination closer to home. Because Dutch campsites are mainly dependent on Dutch and German visitors, they are well positioned to receive these tourists.

Growth is also expected to continue in the long term. The Netherlands Bureau for Tourism and Conventions (NBTC) expects that the Netherlands will have as many as 61 million guests in 2035, an increase of 20 percent compared to 2024. Of these, 28 million will come from abroad. The share of foreigners in the total number of guests continues to increase. It is therefore expected that the number of overnight stays will increase further until 2035.

## Prices continue to rise

Campsite prices appear to be rising this year at a similar pace to previous years, according to data from Booking Experts, a major supplier of holiday rental systems. Although the average price for an overnight stay at a camping site increased relatively rapidly at the beginning of this year, this picture is distorted. The camping season has just started and in May prices are traditionally above the annual average. When corrected for this, it appears that the average price for a camping spot is currently increasing at the same pace.

The price increase is partly because the more luxurious campsites are offering more and more extra facilities. For example, there are a large number of more luxurious campsites that now install a [private sanitary](https://pretwerk.nl/productnieuws/centrale-sanitairgebouwen-steeds-vaker-vervangen-door-prive-sanitair-op-de-camping/83097/) building with a toilet, shower and sometimes even a dishwasher at each pitch.

## Opportunities to attract more campers

To achieve further growth, it is important that campsite owners respond to the changing booking preferences of campers. Free cancellation results in 31 percent more bookings and only 15 percent more cancellations, according to research by [Holidu](https://www.holidu.nl/), an originally German platform for renting holiday accommodations. Making cancellation free also ensures that the guest has less hesitation when booking; it builds trust between the guests and the campsite operator.

Free choice in choosing the check-in day can also increase the number of guests. Friday, Saturday and Monday are the most popular check-in days, but there is also a significant portion who prefer to arrive on a different day. For example, 9 percent of guests check in on Thursday and the same percentage on Sunday, according to research by Holidu. By leaving the choice free, entrepreneurs ensure that guests with different wishes also find a place.

It is also useful to open the booking calendar well in advance. For example, 21 percent of guests book more than six months in advance and 44 percent book between three and six months in advance, according to research by Holidu. A booking calendar that is shorter may discourage returning guests from booking a spot early.

Finally, it is very important to have good, clear photos of the campsite with a clear order and in a balanced number. Research by Holidu shows that twenty to forty photos result in more bookings. However, more than forty photos can overwhelm potential visitors and lead to fewer bookings.

By implementing these strategies, campsites can increase their attractiveness and meet the needs of modern campers.
