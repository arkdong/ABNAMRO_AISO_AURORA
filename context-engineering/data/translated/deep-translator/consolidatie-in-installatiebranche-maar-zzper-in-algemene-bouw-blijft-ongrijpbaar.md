# Consolidation in the installation industry, but self-employment in general construction remains elusive

[Sector forecast](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/sectorprognoses/index.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

09/12/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[Albert Jan Swart](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/albert-jan-swart/index.html)

In their fight against staff shortages, construction companies are using mergers and acquisitions. The resulting economies of scale can ensure higher productivity, for example in the installation sector. The growth of the sector is hampered by the limited granting of permits, including the nitrogen problem.

Growth for the Construction sector is expected to remain at 1.5 percent in 2025. Civil engineering, civil engineering and the installation sector are the fastest growing sectors, partly due to the expansion of the electricity grid and the sustainability of existing construction. Growth may increase to 2.5 percent in 2026 and 2027, partly thanks to construction projects that have been licensed and started since the end of 2024. The expectation for 2027 is still uncertain because government budgets for maintenance and construction of infrastructure have not yet been determined.

Despite lower construction production in 2023 and 2024 and limited growth in 2025, the personnel shortage is still acute. Competition for migrant workers is also increasing, partly due to the growth of the Polish economy. It may become even more difficult to attract migrant workers in the coming years due to German plans for extensive investments in infrastructure, although the question is how quickly the Germans will actually start these projects.

## Consolidation in the installation industry

Although the construction sector has hardly grown and long-term interest rates are still quite high, the number of mergers and acquisitions has been on the rise again since 2024 after a short-term dip in 2023. For example, strong consolidation is underway in the installation sector. The purchasing parties want to realize economies of scale because they can offer a broader package of services and expertise after an acquisition, such as the installation and maintenance of electricity, air conditioning and heat pumps. This can be especially interesting in the utility market because of the long-term maintenance contracts that are common there. Through acquisitions, installation companies actually buy personnel with the right knowledge and skills.

But in a large part of the sector, those highly sought-after employees are quite elusive. In general construction (including contracting), no less than half (52 percent) of workers are self-employed. In other sectors, employment relationships are more structural. In civil engineering, 81 percent of employment is filled by permanent employees, and only 19 percent by self-employed people, including self-employed persons. In specialized construction, which includes the installation sector, permanent employees provide 62 percent of the required labor. In addition to self-employed persons, companies with two employees are currently [on the rise](https://www.cobouw.nl/332751/bedrijvenmonitor-bouwsector-groeit-weer-ueel-ddank-tweemanszaken).

## Taking over contracting difficult

Residential and non-residential construction is dominated by companies that work purely on a project basis and employ independent specialists or specialized subcontractors for each project, who often work for different clients. This makes it difficult, for example, to take over a small contractor with the aim of having more employees. After all, such companies are largely dependent on externally hired skilled workers. Research conducted by Ipsos I&O on behalf of ABN AMRO shows that almost two-thirds (62 percent) of entrepreneurs in the construction sector and industry would like to transfer their company to a new owner within ten years. Three in ten of them (30 percent) want to transfer the company to a family member, and employees are also often (9 percent) in the picture as a possible successor. However, a fairly large proportion of entrepreneurs who want to transfer within ten years are not yet working on the succession (35 percent).

Medium-sized companies in the installation sector in particular, whose numbers are decreasing as a result of takeovers, are popular takeover targets for larger peers. But for small general construction companies, the issue of succession seems difficult for the reasons stated above. Some entrepreneurs will have little choice but to close the business before they retire. That is a shame for the entrepreneur, who will not receive any purchase price, but also for the Dutch economy. The combination of the staff shortage and the low number of mergers and acquisitions in general construction makes it difficult to achieve economies of scale and thus increase productivity. Companies that succeed in retaining employees or making the right acquisitions can benefit. But for a large part of the sector, the traveling self-employed circus remains elusive.

Examples are the nitrogen dossier, the significant price increases for energy and building materials and also the personnel shortages. Driven by trends, innovations and legislation and regulations, the relevance of themes such as sustainability and environmental impact is growing and the sector is looking for a new balance.
