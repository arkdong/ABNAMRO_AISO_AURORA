ABN AMRO Editorial

The draft versions of the Cyber Security Decree and the Resilience of Critical Entities Decree (WKE) are now with the House of Representatives. This represents a next step in strengthening the digital and physical security of vital sectors.

## What's changing?

Many more sectors will soon be supervised under NIS2, such as energy, healthcare, transport, finance and digital infrastructure. Organizations will be given stricter obligations in terms of risk assessment, incident reporting and recovery measures.

## Physical threats also under scrutiny

The WKE decision obliges vital organizations to protect themselves against physical disasters such as sabotage or natural disasters. Careful risk analyzes and preventive measures are becoming the norm.

## Balanced legislation after consultation

The decisions were reached after extensive consultation. The legal texts are clear and enforceable, with clear definitions and demarcations. Now we have to wait for quick political approval.

View the official documents of the [House of Representatives](https://www.tweedekamer.nl/kamerstuks/brief_administratie/detail?id=2025Z13678&did=2025D31013).
