# Cyber hygiene: the basis of digital security

ABN AMRO Editorial

The European NIS2 guideline mentions it nine times: cyber hygiene. That's telling. It is not an option, but an absolute condition for working digitally safely. Entrepreneurs who take cyber hygiene seriously reduce the risk of cyber attacks and lay a strong foundation for sustainable digital resilience.

Cyber ‚Äã‚Äãhygiene revolves around the basic measures that every organization must have in place: strong passwords, multi-factor authentication, regular updates, reliable backups and employee alertness. But it is also about daily habits. Consider immediately reporting suspicious emails, locking your screen when you walk away or avoiding private devices without security.

## The basics are often forgotten

Yet in practice it appears that many organizations make mistakes. Updates are postponed because production is not allowed to stand still, trainees are given too much access or backups are made but not tested. Old accounts of former employees sometimes remain active. People often think: ‚ÄúWe are not interesting for hackers.‚Äù

## Take cyber hygiene seriously

Cyber criminals focus on companies that do not have the basics in order. A forgotten update or a weak password is often enough to get in. And once inside, one infected laptop can shut down an entire organization.

With the NIS2 Quality Mark (NIS2 QM) you show that your organization has properly arranged the basics. The quality mark confirms that you meet the requirements of good cyber hygiene, checked by an independent audit.

## Get your basics in order in 15 measures

The basis of NIS2 QM consists of 15 measures. For comparison: the international standard ISO 27001 contains 93. For many SMEs, those 15 steps already offer more than enough protection to no longer be of interest to cyber criminals.

True digital resilience starts with the basics. Anyone who takes cyber hygiene seriously lays the foundation for sustainable security.
