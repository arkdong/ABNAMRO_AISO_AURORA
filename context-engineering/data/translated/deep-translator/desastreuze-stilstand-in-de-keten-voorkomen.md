# Preventing disastrous halts in the chain

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cybersecurity](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/veilig/index.html)

26/05/2025 • 3 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

From food production to hospital care: cyber attacks are increasingly affecting the vital processes of our society. It is no longer just about data leaks or financial damage. The real risk now is downtime. Downtime in production, logistics or healthcare provision. Standstill of our economy and society.

This threat requires a broad, preventive approach. In crisis management we use the [All Hazards approach](https://samendigitaalveilig.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/All-hazards-examples.jpg) to view all digital and physical risks. This thinking helps organizations improve their protection against various threats, such as ransomware and supply chain disruptions.

## A chain is only as strong as its weakest digital link

Organizations increasingly experience vulnerabilities outside their sphere of influence in an increasingly interconnected economy. A single cyber attack on a supplier can cause a chain reaction with major consequences. The examples below show how vulnerable our processes are – and why chain safety is crucial.

1. Food production halted by cyber attack on glass jar supplier: An attack hits a supplier, which stops supplying glass packaging to the food manufacturer. The result: empty supermarket shelves and millions in reputational damage.
2. Hospital operations postponed due to hacked laundry: A [ransomware](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/wat-is-ransomware.html) attack shuts down a hospital laundry. Hospitals no longer receive sterile sheets, which means operations have to be postponed. The impact on patient safety is immediately noticeable.
3. Hundreds of companies shut down due to hack at IT service provider (MSP): Hackers gain access to the central management systems of a Managed Service Provider. In one fell swoop, hundreds of SMEs come to a standstill. From cash registers to customer data: everything unusable.
4. Logistics software hacked - supply completely disrupted: Malware shuts down the route planning and freight software of a major logistics player. Transports are disrupted, shops remain empty and perishable goods are lost.
5. Production halted due to insecure remote access: A security flaw in remotely managed machines leads to production line sabotage. The damage to equipment and turnover amounts to millions.

## All Hazards: think in scenarios – and take action

Examples show that a cyber attack often affects more than just the affected organization. The impact is chain-wide – and can even lead to social disruption. That is why an [All Hazards approach](https://samendigitaalveilig.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/All-hazards-examples.jpg) is essential: thinking through scenarios, identifying risks, assessing suppliers and taking timely measures.

## The role of the NIS2 guideline in chain security

Essential and important organizations must examine their entire supply chain due to the European NIS2 directive. According to Article 21.2d of the directive, companies must identify risks in the chain and impose security requirements on suppliers. This means a major boost for chain safety in many sectors.
