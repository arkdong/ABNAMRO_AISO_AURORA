# Truly sustainable: HEMA is laying a foundation for the future

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Retail](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/retail/index.html)

28/10/2025 • 6 minutes reading

[Henk Hofstede](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/henk-hofstede/index.html)

HEMA will be celebrating its 100th anniversary in 2026. To remain relevant in the future, the Dutch retailer is fully committed to sustainability as the core of its strategy. “Sustainable policy is future-proof policy,” says sustainability director Marieke de Haan-Doolaard. “That's why it's embedded in everything we do.” HEMA has been nominated for the ABN AMRO Sustainable Retailer Award 2025-2026.

Since 1926, HEMA has been an indispensable part of the Dutch street scene. The retailer has been making products for every moment of daily life for 99 years. According to the store chain, there is also a responsibility there. With its mission 'a better everyday life in a more beautiful world', HEMA is fully committed to sustainability.

## Designing with responsibility

An important part of that course is developing products that are more beautiful, more practical, better and that last longer. “We are of course a retailer,” De Haan-Doolaard explains. “We want to make beautiful products that our customers can enjoy for as long as possible. That is the core, that is our responsibility.”

HEMA has a special position in this: all products are designed in-house. De Haan-Doolaard: “Our designers think along from the start: which materials do we use? What is beautiful, what is strong, what is sustainable? How do I ensure that a product lasts as long as possible?” Well-known examples are the grow-along rompers, which last two sizes thanks to a double row of press studs, or the reusable wrapping paper.

> “We want to make beautiful products that our customers can enjoy for as long as possible. That is the core, that is our responsibility.”

Marieke de Haan-Doolaard

Director of Sustainability HEMA

According to De Haan-Doolaard, the choice of materials is also carefully considered. “We have a materials library in which we look at the type of product, the raw materials used and the impact on CO₂. Suppose we design a vase. Do you choose glass or porcelain? What does that mean for the footprint? And for the price? This way our designers can make a well-founded decision in terms of quality, sustainability and affordability.”

## Affordability and sustainability in balance

Because affordability is an essential aspect for HEMA. “It is important that sustainable products are not only good for the environment, but also remain accessible and affordable for the consumer,” says De Haan-Doolaard. "Sometimes that balance works out perfectly. Such as with the grow-along romper. Smart for the environment and smart for the wallet."

But that combination is not always easy. De Haan-Doolaard gives the multifunctional bicycle light as an example. "An amazing product. Rechargeable, designed to be used both on the bike and while running and with white and red light. It hit the shelves three years ago, but it turned out to be too expensive and was not sold. That was a painful realization." Yet HEMA did not stop. Together with suppliers, solutions were sought to reduce the price without losing sight of quality. Three years later, there is now an affordable multifunctional lamp in stores. “Because we control the process ourselves, we can take one step back and two steps forward,” she explains.

> “It is important that sustainable products are not only good for the environment, but also remain accessible and affordable for consumers.”

Marieke de Haan-Doolaard

Director of Sustainability HEMA

The packaging of the Christmas baubles also turned out to be a learning point. “We wanted more sustainable packaging,” she says. "So last year we replaced the plastic with a completely cardboard box. Beautiful, but customers could no longer see the Christmas baubles and so it did not sell. We thus exceeded our goal."

## Working on a stable chain

Although HEMA designs all products in-house, they are produced worldwide. The retailer has four purchasing offices in Asia. An enormous luxury, according to De Haan-Doolaard. "We have built up long-term relationships with suppliers. There is a Corporate Social Responsibility counterpart at each office who focuses entirely on compliance and improvement at the factories. Our colleague in Bangladesh, for example, has been there for twelve years and knows the factories inside and out. That really makes a difference."

## Mutual responsibility is the basis

Long-term partnerships with suppliers are essential for de Haan-Doolaard. "We want to continue selling good products for a long time to come. This is only possible if you maintain strong, sustainable relationships with suppliers. We have been working with many suppliers for ten years. Good relationships demonstrably lead to higher productivity, better quality and future-proofing. That is pure business logic."

HEMA believes that good relationships are based on mutual responsibility. For example, in 2024 the retailer introduced a Supplier Code of Conduct. This so-called two-way code of conduct not only states what HEMA expects from suppliers, but also what they can expect from HEMA. “We also take our own responsibility,” De Haan-Doolaard explains. “For example, we keep an eye on the timeline when implementing improvements, so that there is not too much pressure on a supplier.”

## Classic sustainable dilemmas

Yet there are also dilemmas. New legislation places more and more obligations on the chain. “We want to include our suppliers in this and help them meet additional requirements. But if legislation changes in the meantime, that can be difficult,” says De Haan-Doolaard. “I find that worrying for our chain partners. That is why we choose our own course and do what feels good for HEMA and our suppliers.”

## Widely supported within HEMA

What is De Haan-Doolaard most proud of? “Sustainability is at the core of HEMA,” she explains. "It is really part of our strategy and is widely supported at the top of our organization. We have a monthly ESG committee that includes 4 MT members, including the CEO and CFO. We are convinced that sustainable policy is also future-proof policy. The fact that we can make that link in the organization is very valuable. And I also see that it is paying off."

> “Sustainability is at the core of HEMA. It is really part of our strategy and widely supported at the top of our organization.”

Marieke de Haan-Doolaard

Director of Sustainability HEMA

The sustainability director mentions HEMA's responsible exit strategy as an example. If the retailer does not continue with a supplier, for example because a product is being discontinued, the collaboration is not abruptly terminated, but carefully phased out with due regard for the human rights of the employees involved. "A buyer recently came to me with the question of how we can carefully go through this process with a supplier. If a buyer comes up with this himself, then I burst with pride."

## Measuring is knowing

Next year HEMA will celebrate its 100th anniversary. How does De Haan-Doolaard see the future? “Sustainability stands or falls with measurability,” she emphasizes. "We can measure the impact increasingly better and manage accordingly. No assumptions, but really looking at what works. And being transparent to stakeholders. You can already see that on the environmental side. You can calculate CO₂ emissions, and we do that. But you now see the same transition on the social side: how do you measure the impact on employees? I think that is a very cool development."

## Dream for the sector

De Haan-Doolaard looks further than its own organization. "My dream is that measurable impact will help the entire retail sector move forward. If we turn a button, but no one else participates, then it makes less sense. We need uniformity and scale. Only if we as a sector use the same information can we really make an impact."
