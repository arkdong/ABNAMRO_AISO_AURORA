# A cyber attack is a financial crisis

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cyber attack](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/cyberattack/index.html)

29/09/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

A cyber attack is not just an IT problem. It has direct consequences for the finances of your company. From small businesses to multinationals, everyone is at risk. An attack could lead to liquidity problems or even bankruptcy.

The direct costs of a cyber incident add up quickly. Think of IT research, legal assistance and crisis communication. Sometimes you also have to replace systems or pay a ransom. In addition, downtime causes loss of turnover. Customer orders are left behind, contracts are not fulfilled and fines may follow. Production and services often come to a standstill, which increases the damage.

## Reputational damage and less trust

A cyber attack also damages the company's reputation. Customers lose confidence and choose the competitor. New deals are not forthcoming, and you may need to offer discounts to win back customers. This reduces your profit margin. Investors are also reacting: share prices are falling and valuations for acquisitions are becoming lower.

## Fines and claims

Supervisors can impose fines for violations of privacy and cybersecurity rules. Business partners and customers can submit claims for damages. Legal proceedings and audits add to the financial pressure. Delivery problems within the chain cause additional costs and liability.

## Structural measures needed

Cyber insurance does not always cover all costs. Premiums may increase after an event and crucial costs may be excluded from the policy. Entrepreneurs must therefore continue to invest in IT, security and compliance.
