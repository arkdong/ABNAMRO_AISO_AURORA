# Only cooperation will accelerate the inland shipping transition

[Interview](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/interviews/index.html)

[Transport & Logistics](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/logistiek/index.html)

04/11/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[Bart Banning](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/bart-banning/index.html)

More intimate cooperation between all parties involved is necessary, especially in these uncertain times.' ABN AMRO advocates more decisiveness between charterers, shippers, skippers, financiers and the government. Only then will the ecosystem work to make inland shipping future-proof.'

“We mainly offer certainty and reliability with our financing,” says Ard-Jan Vergunst. He and his colleague Bart Banning emphasize that they want to build a world that is also future-proof from a logistics point of view.

Banning: 'And then it is important that we hear very clearly from our customers how they feel about this theme in the competition.'

> “Investments in inland shipping always have a long-term character.”

Ard Vergunst

Business Banker Inland Shipping at ABN AMRO

## Inland shipping invests with a long-term focus

The bank wants to give greater weight to the interests of future generations in decision-making. ABN Amro describes this goal in English as 'Banking for better, for generations to come'. This fits in well with inland shipping, which often involves investments lasting decades. Vergunst: 'And that means that with banking you don't just look at the present day, but actually always look ahead for a longer period.'

Banning is a Transport and Logistics sector expert at the bank. 'I am expected to be able to explain developments and trends in, among other things, the inland shipping sector.' Vergunst is a Business Banker in Inland Shipping at ABN Amro and has daily contact with customers in that sector.

## Which motorization is appropriate?

In inland shipping, the vast majority of entrepreneurs only have one ship. The financing is long-term and must be appropriate. Vergunst: 'The ship owner mainly wants security and reliability, which makes it more difficult to pioneer a new technology. But we see that inland shipping is using new drives. If you have a new ship built, you can of course only make a choice once. Am I going to build that ship with a Stage V diesel engine, with HVO blending? Or do I opt for a hybrid drive, so that I have the choice to adjust the motorization in the future? Or am I now going to take the step towards a completely zero-emission ship? We will discuss this decision with the customer.'

Banning: 'Technological development has gone so fast in recent years that there are variants of which one has already been really proven, but the other has not yet. As long as that is the case, we will continue to see that new engines with a modular system are often chosen.'

> “In the medium term, there will be increasing transport volumes.”

Bart Banning

Sector Banker Transport & Logistics

## Uncertain world with opportunities

The burden on inland navigation skippers is increasing, but the prospects for the future seem favorable, say Vergunst and Banning. Vergunst: 'Sustainability is just one theme. We are dealing with transport flows in the world that are changing, partly due to American import tariffs. We see lower water levels in the Rhine, personnel costs have risen enormously. There are all kinds of things going on and that demands a lot from such an inland shipping entrepreneur.'

Banning: 'But if you look in the medium term, everyone is positive about the rising volumes in freight transport. The port of Rotterdam, for example, says that it will handle approximately 50% extra containers between now and 2035, around 8 million TEU. The hope is that a large part of this will go via inland shipping, which still has space, unlike road transport.'

> “Inland shipping needs a well-thought-out label system.”

Bart Banning

Sector Banker Transport & Logistics

## Logistics ecosystem is all about collaboration

Collaboration is the key to achieving successful improvements faster. Banning: 'We call this the logistics ecosystem surrounding inland shipping. These are all stakeholders who benefit from a healthy inland shipping sector. The skippers benefit from the clients doing as much as possible via inland shipping and the clients benefit from the skippers transporting the goods efficiently and cleanly. Shippers, politicians and the financial sector are also involved. If the ecosystem starts to work closely together, its potential will be utilised.'

Banning: 'An example of the ecosystem is to jointly further activate the existing emission label system. The Dutch emissions system can be an example for the emissions system at European level. A well-thought-out label system provides insight into the way in which the inland shipping sector carries out logistics for shippers, insurers, banks and port companies, leading to financial benefits that benefit the business model.' Vergunst: 'Everyone bears their own responsibility and has a contribution to that ecosystem.'
