# Facta: always on the move, even when it comes to Gen Zers

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

22/05/2025 • 5 minutes reading

[David Kemps](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-kemps/index.html)

Companies have been able to count on Facta for years for service and maintenance of rotating equipment, electrical and construction technology. The organization has recently taken significant steps: with new business units, more complete services and larger projects. This has an impact on staff and the recruitment of new talent.

How does [Facta](https://www.factarotating.nl/) deal with this, while at the same time having to learn to work with Gen Z? We spoke to general manager Jeffrey Schotvanger and his colleague Jaap Zijlstra from the Construction Technology business unit. 'The youngest generation really expects different things from an employer. It is important to go along with this if you want to attract and retain them.'

## A look at Generation Z with practical training

This article is part of a study by ABN AMRO into the youngest generation in the labor market: Generation Z, born between 1997 and 2012. In our research we specifically focus on those with a practical education. What do they find important in their work and what are they looking for in an employer? The message that emerges from our report is clear. Practically educated Gen Zers especially want:

1. Clarity about salary and contract form
2. Knowing what concrete work they will do and how they can grow
3. That the basics are in order: good equipment and professional guidance

Curious about our full analysis? Read the research report here: [What practically educated Gen Zers really want](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/wat-praktisch-opgeleide-gen-zers-echt-willen.html)

## Opportunities and challenges through growth

Thanks to the broad expertise, the type of work at Facta is different than before, according to Jeffrey. 'Twenty years ago, customers came to our workshop. Now we usually go to our customers and often take over the role of a technical service. We carry out large, complex projects on location. This requires more than just technical skills: project management, work preparation and inspections are also on the agenda.'

> “We offer plenty of further development opportunities for our employees and that is a useful weapon in recruitment.”

Jaap Zijlstra  
Relationship manager Facta

## An eye for development

“The growth we have experienced in recent years has influenced our HR and recruitment policy,” says Jeffrey. 'Our slogan is Always on the move. That applies to Facta, but we expect the same from our people.' Jaap: 'We offer them challenge, variety and future prospects. Are you tired of welding in the workshop? Then you can, for example, switch to the service department, or retrain for another business unit at our company school. In short: there are plenty of opportunities for further development within this company, and that is also a useful weapon in recruitment.'

> “We think it is important that everything is well organised: attractive and challenging work, company clothing, buses with good tools. Autonomy and freedom are also highly valued.”

Jeffrey Schotvanger  
General Manager Facta

## Everything arranged down to the last detail

Jeffrey and Jaap often hear that Facta is a cool company to work for. To a large extent, this is due to the attention they give their people. 'We think it is important that everything is well organised: attractive and challenging work, company clothing, buses with good tools. Autonomy and freedom are also highly valued,” says Jeffrey. Jaap adds: 'In addition, our employment conditions are above average, we invest a lot of time in onboarding new colleagues and we encourage a pleasant, family atmosphere in the workplace. Jeffrey still visits all branches every month to stay connected to the work floor. People feel that personal attention.'

## New generation on the floor

No matter how well Facta's employer brand is, it is not easy to attract young people. Jeffrey: 'Gen Zers – born between 1996 and 2010 – have different wishes than previous generations. For example, there is a good chance that they will want to go to Australia or Spain for another year in between. You have to be open to that as an employer, otherwise you won't keep them on board.' Jaap: 'They also seem less concerned with making a lot of money. In the past, if you asked who wanted to work overtime, your fingers would shoot up in the air. Now they find a good work-life balance more important.'

## Experience and education? Rather, look at character

It has therefore become more difficult to recruit suitable employees. Because of the difference between generations, but according to Jaap there is an even more important cause: 'The shortage of job seekers. We do not fill any of our positions easily. That is why we founded a business school, where we train people technically ourselves. This allows us to search differently: less focus on numbers and skills, but on affinity and work attitude. For example, we can now also focus on car mechanics. They often do not yet have the right technical skills, but we teach them on the job.'

## Ties with trainers

Facta focuses partly on school leavers and lateral entrants. This turns out to be a good strategy, thanks to the close ties the company has with schools. “We have a lot of BBL and BOL students walking around, so they are already working while they are still going to school,” says Jaap. 'We see that MBO is not sufficiently connected to practice. We invest a lot of time in closing that gap, for example with a substantive contribution to learning programs. This gives us more brand awareness among students. Furthermore, we believe that schools and businesses should work together to accelerate technological development and prepare the professionals of tomorrow. We are doing our best to achieve that.'

## Want to captivate, attract and retain Gen Zers? These are Facta's tips:

- Offer sufficient variety and challenge in the work.
- Ensure good employment and fringe conditions: from salary, working hours and work equipment to culture.
- Listen to the needs of young applicants and employees.
- Lower the bar in your vacancies: what is really needed, and what do they learn on the job?
- Collaborate with trainers.
- Your own staff are your ambassadors: you find the best people through others.
- Gen Z is brand conscious. Build and invest in your brand awareness, image and culture.
