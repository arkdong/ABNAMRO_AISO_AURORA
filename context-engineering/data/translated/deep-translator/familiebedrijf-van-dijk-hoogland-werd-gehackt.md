# Family business Van Dijk Hoogland was hacked

[Entrepreneurial Stories](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/ondernemershistories/index.html)

[Cyber attack](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/cyberattack/index.html)

23/06/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

What do a company involved in earthmoving, agricultural technology and contract work and cybercrime have in common? Basically nothing at all, you would think. Yet Van Dijk Hoogland BV was hacked a few years ago. “It happened in the middle of the day,” says owner Danielle van Dijk.

“The screen went black, we couldn't do anything anymore.” Very soon there was a demand for ransom, but the [earthmoving and agricultural technology company](https://www.vandijkhoogland.nl/) did not pay it. Fortunately, a good backup system provided the solution, but it was a real shock.

Security for SMEs is extremely important, according to Gerwin Otten of [Cumela](https://www.cumela.nl/); the trade association for green, land and infrastructure. "No one is against safety, but we have to look carefully at what is appropriate for each company. A chip builder in Eindhoven must take different measures than a green company in the region."

## Cybercriminals demand ransom after hack

In the Digital Safe Entrepreneurship video series by Samen Digitaal Veilig, Danielle shares her experience with the hackers. Since then, the company has joined forces with an external IT partner who takes care of security for them.
