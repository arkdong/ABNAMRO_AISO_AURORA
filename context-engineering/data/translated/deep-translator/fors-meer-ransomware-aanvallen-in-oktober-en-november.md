ABN AMRO Editorial

The number of ransomware attacks worldwide has increased significantly. In October alone, 594 incidents were recorded. This amounts to an increase of 41 percent compared to September.

The industrial sector was hardest hit in October, with 167 ransomware attacks (28 percent of the total). The retail and car manufacturers sectors followed with 124 incidents. The healthcare sector recorded 64 ransomware attacks.

## Ransomware threat groups

The ransomware group Qilin remained the most active activist group in October with 170 attacks (29 percent of the total).Â This was followed by Sinobi (65 attacks, 15 percent) and Akira (64 attacks, also 15 percent).

In October we enter one of the most active times of the year. Vigilance is more important than ever. Ransomware activity is increasing and large-scale attacks continue to cause economic and operational damage.

## Consequences for companies

For companies in the Netherlands, these figures mean that the peak season for cyber threats has begun. There is a clear pattern of more targeted and extensive ransomware campaigns that capitalize on increased consumer activity (Black Friday, Cyber â€‹â€‹Monday, holidays).

It is therefore very important that companies take immediate action. Consider:

- Focusing and practicing incident and crisis response plans.
- Perform proactive monitoring of systems and networks.
- Training employees in cyber awareness, focusing on ransomware triggers.
- Ensuring reliable, up-to-date backups and recovery procedures.
