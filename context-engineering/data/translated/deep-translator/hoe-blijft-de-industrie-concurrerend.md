# How does the industry stay competitive?

[Podcast](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/in-spraak/podcast/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

29/09/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[David Kemps](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-kemps/index.html)

Under pressure from rising energy costs, personnel shortages and global competition, more and more companies are considering moving their production abroad. How do you keep companies within the industrial sector, which accounts for 12% of GDP, future-proof?

## Servitization as a weapon against competition

Listen to the latest episode of the podcast 'Progress at the table' (August 2025) by Berenschot. This podcast and vodcast was previously published on Berenschot's [website](https://www.berenschot.nl/podcasts/podcast-5-hoe-blijft-de-nederlandse-industrie-concurrerend).

Industry leaders are sounding the alarm. Are new revenue models the answer? According to industry experts David Kemps of ABN AMRO and Prof. Meindert Flikkema of Berenschot, yes. They discuss this in the latest episode of the podcast 'Progress at the table', presented by Rens de Jong.

According to Flikkema, high labor costs, expensive energy and scarcity of raw materials are not the only problem. Many industrial companies, especially SMEs, lack a long-term vision. “Many entrepreneurs are concerned with today and tomorrow, but lack a clear strategy for the next five to ten years,” says Kemps.

> “We need to move towards a model in which you not only sell a machine, but offer the certainty that it will always continue to run.”

Prof. Meindert Flikkema

## From products to services

According to both experts, one of the solutions lies in combining products and services. “We need to move towards a model in which you not only sell a machine, but offer the certainty that it will always continue to run,” says Flikkema. Such a Product-as-a-Service model provides more added value and long-term customer relationships, but according to Kemps "also requires a completely different way of financing and organizing".

“We are an innovative country with top universities. That is where our strength lies, we must make better use of that ingenuity,” says Flikkema. Customers' problems and ideas can be the key to new revenue models. Although many people think of big names such as ASML or Aalberts when they think of industry, more than 95% of the sector consists of SME companies. This ecosystem forms the backbone of the Dutch economy. “No services without production,” says Kemps. “Our country cannot only run on lawyers, notaries and... bankers.”

## Drivers' turn

What can drivers do tomorrow? According to Kemps, it starts with insight into existing customers and machines. "Find out where your machines are located and see if you can conclude maintenance contracts. That can provide enormous value." Flikkema adds: “Involve your customer in the conversation. Their problems and ideas can be the key to new revenue models.” In short, the industry does not necessarily have to shrink, but it does have to transform. Less focus on costs and more attention to innovation, customer value and collaboration are a prerequisite for making the sector future-proof.

Or, as Flikkema puts it: “The industry does not have to end up in winter, provided companies take innovative steps now.”
