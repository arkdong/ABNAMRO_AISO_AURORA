# How retailers can lose or maintain their visibility due to AI

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Retail](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/retail/index.html)

17/07/2025 • 5 minutes reading

[Henk Hofstede](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/henk-hofstede/index.html)

The way consumers search for products is changing significantly due to the use of AI assistants. As a result, retailers have less control over the customer journey and the dependence on data that AI uses to make choices is increasing.

The way consumers search for products is changing drastically. Previously, Google was the gateway to the internet, but today we are seeing a strong shift towards AI assistants. These tools formulate answers themselves, make recommendations and even direct purchases. For retailers, this means less control over the customer journey, less website traffic and greater dependence on the data that AI uses to make choices. This blog describes what this development means in concrete terms and how a retailer can respond to it.

## The changing customer journey: from clicks to answers

To date, consumers shopping online started with a Google search, followed by clicking on a few websites, comparing products and hopefully making a purchase. AI tools such as Google AI Overview, ChatGPT, and Perplexity largely take this work off the hands of the consumer.

> “The AI ​​assistant then provides ready-made advice about the best options, based on price, quality, customer reviews, quality marks and available data, without the consumer having to visit websites themselves.”

Henk Hofstede

Sector Banker Retail

Suppose a consumer is looking for a home sound system. Where he used to look for a 'home sound system', he now searches via AI assistants for a 'sound system for the entire house, with a living area of ​​150 m², spread over four rooms, with a simple installation and a maximum budget of 2,000 euros'. The AI ​​assistant then provides ready-made advice about the best options, based on price, quality, customer reviews, quality marks and available data, without the consumer having to visit websites themselves.

This has major consequences for the retailer. Direct website visits decrease because the AI ​​already presents the information to the consumer. In addition, the retailer loses influence on the customer journey and brand experience, because the AI ​​determines which product, brand or store is shown. The competition is increasing because the retailer is competing with all products that objectively score better on the desired criteria based on the available data.

## AI determines what the consumer sees

AI assistants mainly use information that is freely accessible on the internet, such as web pages, product descriptions, reviews and ratings. Sometimes it concerns plain text, sometimes it concerns structured product information that is specially designed so that AI systems can easily read it. Some AI systems obtain information from public product and quality mark databases.

> “In short, AI relies on what is publicly discoverable, structured and credible.”

Henk Hofstede

Sector Banker Retail

Direct links with brands or retailers are currently still rare. However, things are moving in that direction with, for example, the new ChatGPT Shopping. It is integrated with Shopify stores via a plugin that makes real-time product information available. Shopify stores are online stores of large and smaller retailers worldwide that are built on the easy-to-use Shopify platform.

In short, AI relies on what is publicly discoverable, structured and credible. Does the retailer not have its product information in order? Then there is a good chance that AI will simply skip its products.

## Risks for the retailer

Risks for the retailer The rise of AI-based search engines leads to a number of concrete risks for retailers, such as the risk of invisibility where products are not mentioned if the data is incorrect or does not meet AI standards. Price pressure is also a risk if AI only selects for the lowest price, leaving out a brand with more quality but a higher price. In addition, the retailer will miss out on crucial customer data if the purchase by the AI ​​assistant is not made via the retailer but via an external platform such as Zalando or Amazon if they have better findable data. This is also called zero-click commerce. Finally, there is a danger of fragmentation of the brand experience, because AI determines how it is presented and, for example, makes neutral, price and/or sustainable recommendations, which reduces customer loyalty.

> “Finally, there is a danger of fragmentation of the brand experience, because AI determines how it is presented.”

Henk Hofstede

Sector Banker Retail

## Promising measures for the retailer

Yet there are smart ways retailers can arm themselves against this AI revolution to maintain visibility and control:

1. Write extensive, unique and complete product content - This is the basics and can be in the form of text or images. This includes well-structured product data on the website, with detailed descriptions, specifications and important certifications, but also how and why the product is used. This must demonstrate that the retailer is a 'product expert'. The AI ​​assistant links the user question to the information on a website or product page and sees this as the source of the answer.
2. Add customer reviews - By adding customer experiences to the website, the reliability of the information provided increases. The AI ​​assistant searches for such sources.
3. Use experts - Show short videos in which professionals demonstrate the use and benefits of a product. This shows the retailer that he or she is an expert in a specific product area. The AI ​​assistant searches for such images and bases its choices on this.
4. Invest in your own AI assistant - If a retailer wants to keep the customer journey in their own hands, their own AI integration is a smart move. Let customers talk to your own AI chatbot on your own website that recommends products, answers questions and processes orders. Integrate this assistant with your own CRM system and inventory and loyalty programs for a hyper-personal service. There's also potential to invest in a Google Lens-like tool in the native app. Customers can compare photos of products they see and like elsewhere with similar products that the retailer has in its range.
5. Focus on brand preference and community - AI assistants make rational choices, but people also make emotional choices and not all purchases will go through AI assistants. Therefore, invest in strong brand stories and transparency about sustainability, origin and quality of the product. By doing community building, building relationships through social media or loyalty programs, the retailer can remain distinctive and maintain a bond with those fans who continue to come to him directly. Loyal customers continue to return to strong brands with recognizable stories.

## AI control is the future

The future of online retail is increasingly driven by AI and we are only at the beginning. This means fewer clicks, more direct answers, more convenience for the customer and less room for the retailer to make itself visible. Poor or incomplete product information ultimately leads to the invisibility of the product. But with well-structured product data, strategic visibility and its own smart AI approach, a retailer can not only survive, but even benefit from this new reality.
