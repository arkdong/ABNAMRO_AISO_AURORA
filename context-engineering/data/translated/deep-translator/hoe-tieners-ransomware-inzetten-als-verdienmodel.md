# How teenagers use ransomware as a revenue model

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cyber attack](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/cyberattack/index.html)

21/07/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

The world of cybercrime is changing rapidly. We first thought about adult hackers in dark rooms. Nowadays, young teenagers, sometimes not yet eighteen years old, use ransomware to extort money from companies. For them, hacking is not a game – they see it as a business.

## A lucrative revenue model

[Ransomware](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/wat-is-ransomware.html) is malicious software that young cyber criminals use to make money. Young people with technical aptitude can find simple tools and manuals online, with which they can quickly carry out attacks. Some of them earn thousands of euros per month. They target small businesses without proper security and demand ransoms in cryptocurrency. They often remain beyond the reach of investigative services.

## From curiosity to extortion

What starts as a curious exploration of digital systems, in some cases results in organized extortion. Young people read other people's success stories on forums, Discord groups or Telegram chats and get seduced. Before they know it, they are participating in a network that spreads ransomware, targets and distributes revenue.

## Why young people in particular?

There are several reasons why young people are so susceptible to this criminal path:

- Financial temptation: The prospect of making a lot of money quickly is irresistible, especially for young people with no income.
- Easy access: Software and explanations are available free or cheaply. Tools are offered ready to use.
- Anonymity and underestimation: Many young perpetrators think they are elusive. They do not realize that IP addresses, payment trails and chat logs can be traced.
- Lack of guidance: Young people with a talent for IT do not always receive the right guidance or examples of ethical use of their skills.

## The consequences are serious

The financial return seems attractive, but the consequences are severe. Young people who are caught not only have to deal with criminal law, but put their entire future at risk:

- No Certificate of Good Conduct (VOG).
- Exclusion from certain studies or internships.
- Problems with international travel or visa applications.
- Reputational damage in a digital world that forgets nothing.
