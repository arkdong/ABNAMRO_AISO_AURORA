# ICT sector and NIS2: take action on time

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cybersecurity](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/veilig/index.html)

16/06/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

The ICT sector is central to the NIS2 guideline, which will come into effect from the third quarter. These European regulations oblige companies to get their cybersecurity in order. Nearly 100,000 ICT companies in the Netherlands, of which more than 93,500 ICT service providers, are affected by this. Yet many companies have not yet started preparations.

## Growth sector with great responsibility

The ICT sector is growing rapidly according to Statistics Netherlands. Between 2011 and 2023 there was an average annual growth of 4.6 percent, with a peak of 9.8 percent in 2023. Companies involved in software development, network management and cybersecurity solutions are particularly on the rise. Of the almost 100,000 ICT companies, approximately 39,000 employ staff. More than 58 percent consist of self-employed people without employees (self-employed).

## Why NIS2 has a direct impact

Because almost every ICT company plays a role in the digital chain, the chain responsibility from NIS2 also applies to them. Customers will increasingly demand proof that ICT suppliers work cyber securely. Without measures, companies risk losing orders.

## Action now = benefit later

The NIS2 guideline sets requirements for risk analyses, security measures and supplier management. This takes time to implement properly. Anyone who starts now will be ready on time and build trust with customers.

Then you are already well on your way and you only have to implement the measures that are not included in ISO27001, but are mandatory under NIS2.
