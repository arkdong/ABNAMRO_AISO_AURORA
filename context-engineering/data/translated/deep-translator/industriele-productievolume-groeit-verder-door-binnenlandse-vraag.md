# Industrial production volume continues to grow due to domestic demand

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

01/10/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[David Kemps](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-kemps/index.html)

The Nevi Purchasing Managers Index for Dutch industry rose further in September from 51.9 to 53.7. Despite few new export orders due to ongoing uncertainty surrounding trade policy, industrial production volume increased due to domestic demand and additional staff had to be hired quickly.

Activity in Dutch industry grew again in September. Inventories of finished products have been further reduced and the sub-indicator for new orders rose sharply to a reading of 53.9. Unfortunately, the number of new export orders is declining. Discussions with Dutch entrepreneurs show that this is mainly due to increased price competition from Asia. The European market has become more important for many Chinese manufacturers due to high US import duties.

Production growth does not apply to all industrial subsectors. The port of Rotterdam is anticipating an exodus now that BP also announced last week that it would stop developing a new biofuels factory. Shell previously announced that it would stop the construction of a biofuel factory in Pernis that had already started. Regulatory pressure, network congestion, nitrogen problems and intense foreign price competition for biofuels are cited as the main reasons for canceling the proposed investments.

In addition to chemicals and plastics recycling, the short-term prospects for the semiconductor and medical industries are also not positive. ASM International lowered its turnover forecast for the fourth quarter and ASML has also indicated that the previously indicated growth forecast for 2026 will probably have to be adjusted downwards due to macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainties.

## Mixed signals from Germany

Many Dutch entrepreneurs keep a close eye on developments in the German economy. After all, the German government has made a lot of money available for improving the infrastructure and scaling up production in the defense sector. This will ultimately create additional demand among Dutch suppliers of metal and plastic products, electrical engineering and building materials. The German statistics agency Destatis surprised with the report that industrial production had increased by 1.3 percent in July, with the largest production increases in mechanical engineering (9.5 percent) and the pharmaceutical industry (8.4 percent). Even the automotive sector showed a small production growth of 2.3 percent compared to June.

This positive trend in July is unfortunately not supported by the Flash HCOB Purchasing Managers Index for the German manufacturing and services sector. In anticipation of the final index, it reached a level of 52.4 on September 23. This is the highest level in the past sixteen months. However, it is only the services sector that is showing rapid improvement in Germany, mainly due to the start-up of new projects. The German industry index actually deteriorated in September from 49.8 to 48.5, due to the lack of new (export) orders.

## Recovery remains uncertain

Domestic investments are picking up again and the production figures of the German economy for July also show slight growth. The recovery of the industry remains uncertain in the short term. Trump's tariffs are limiting European exports and many manufacturers of machinery, chemicals and plastic packaging are experiencing stiff price competition from China. In the long term, European governments can strengthen the industry with investments in defense and infrastructure.

## Read more in the industrial sector

The industry is a heavyweight in the Dutch economy. Export is an important driver for this, but at the same time creates uncertainty. There are also themes that have a positive influence on growth, such as cooperation in the chain, innovation and sustainability.
