# Innovation without scaling up is occupational therapy

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

26/08/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Leontien de Waal](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/leontien-de-waal/index.html)

Fresh back from holiday, I see it again on social media: a call from the government to register your innovation that can accelerate housing construction. A program has made several million euros available for this purpose. Good news? Maybe.

## Labor productivity requires scaling

The fact is that labor productivity in construction has been stagnating for years. While sectors such as logistics, industry and even healthcare digitalize and optimize their processes, labor productivity in construction fell by 2 percent between 2019 and 2023, according to the Economic Institute for Construction. And while construction does not stand still. In fact, the sector is buzzing with innovations and inspiration. From apps to robots, from AI-driven maintenance to biobased construction products, applications tumble over each other at every trade fair and in every innovation program. But here's the problem: innovations are no guarantee for productivity. For this they also have to be scaled up.

> “Innovations are no guarantee for productivity”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction

## Pitfall of even more pilots

And that's exactly where it stops. New solutions are promoted through an acceleration program, but are not always applied more widely. Instead of moving forward, we get stuck in pilots, living labs and demonstration projects. Governments, investors and regional development agencies enthusiastically stimulate innovations. There is money for solutions that focus on energy efficiency, predictive maintenance with AI and water-conscious construction. Well-intentioned, but it does not yet result in structural acceleration.

The result is lack of productivity growth, which should actually increase with the help of innovation. Especially at the moment, when companies are reconsidering their investments due to economic and geopolitical uncertainty and are still struggling with failure costs, duplication of effort and sub-optimal planning. Then it is risky to allow extra (public) money to spread over new initiatives without structural follow-up. That money easily disappears into a swamp of reports, meetings and draft reviews. The sector knows this better than anyone else.

> “Guarantee sufficient market volume to make investments profitable”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction

## Encourage implementation

For real progress we need to invest in large-scale application and rollout over at least ten projects. Help construction entrepreneurs to make their people more digitally skilled. Support manufacturers in scaling up production. Guarantee sufficient market volume to make investments profitable. A call to governments, development companies and investment funds: stop subsidizing new innovations. Join forces and resources more often. Encourage implementation rather than inspiration. Do not create more 'pots', but provide more construction volume.

## Read more in the construction sector

The Dutch construction sector has been one of the best performing sectors in recent years, despite several challenges facing the sector. Examples are the nitrogen dossier, the significant price increases for energy and building materials and also the personnel shortages. Driven by trends, innovations and legislation and regulations, the relevance of themes such as sustainability and environmental impact is growing and the sector is looking for a new balance.
