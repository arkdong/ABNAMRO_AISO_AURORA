The search and book behavior of Generation Z is significantly different from previous generations. It appears that this young generation makes more use of physical travel agencies and more often of AI to receive advice when booking their holiday.

Travel agencies have been doing well in recent years. Since the corona pandemic, ABN AMRO has seen a strong recovery in the industry. The pandemic was followed by several years of strong growth due to pent-up demand following global travel restrictions. The 12-month average is starting to approach the level that would have been reached if pre-2020 growth had continued (Figure 1). This is striking, because the strong inflation and the storm of AI applications have apparently not had a negative impact on the turnover figures of travel agencies.

## Shorter booking period

However, changes have occurred in recent years, such as travelers booking their trips later. The peak in the third quarter, during which the summer holidays fall, has become a lot higher. At the same time, turnover in the fourth and first quarters is falling faster than before. The fact that travelers book later is the result of more uncertainty in the world, such as war in Ukraine, unrest in Palestine and political tensions elsewhere in the world. The higher turnover in the third quarter may also be the result of a shift in expenditure from winter holidays to summer holidays, although there is no clear indication of this.

Part of the increase in turnover can be explained by the fact that the prices of many of the underlying products and services have become more expensive. For example, prices for airline tickets, hotels, holiday parks and car rental have risen worldwide. These are usually included in the prices charged by travel agencies.

However, despite the increased prices, consumers are choosing not to avoid travel agencies and to arrange their own travel. Research by the Netherlands Bureau for Tourism & Conventions (NBTC) shows that the number of people who obtain information for their trip via a physical travel agency has increased from 6 percent to 8 percent between April 2024 and July 2025. At the same time, online travel organizations and booking websites also see their popularity increase slightly, from 51 to 53 percent. Travelers therefore use more sources to arrive at their ideal holiday plan.

## Young people use physical travel agencies more often

The noticeable increase in physical travel agencies is largely because they are better able to attract the younger age groups (18-24 years and 25-34 years). These age groups obtain information from the physical travel agency more often than before. Respectively, this has increased for these groups from 5 to 12 percent and from 3 to 10 percent, according to a comparison of the figures from the NBTC's Holiday Sentiment Monitor from April 2024 with July 2025.

The popularity of the physical travel agency among younger generations is now even higher than among older generations. While between 5 and 7 percent of the groups 35-44, 45-54 and 55-64 years choose physical travel agencies as a source of information, this is 12 and 10 percent respectively for 18-24 and 25-34 years. Only the age group over 65 also makes extensive use of physical travel agencies, at 11 percent. However, online travel organizations are less popular among these elderly people than among young people.

An earlier [American survey](https://www.ibsplc.com/news/survey-surprising-trends-among-the-youngest-travelers-as-47-of-americans-turn-toward-travel-agents-to-book-their-holiday-travel) shows that as many as 38 percent of millennials and Gen Z actually book through traditional travel agents. This is in stark contrast to Gen X and Boomers, only 12 and 2 percent of whom book through a traditional travel agent. The study also shows that Gen X prefers online travel agencies when booking their holiday trips (35 percent).

The external appearance of the physical travel agency has changed. Where previously these were mainly shops in the local shopping centre, these are now increasingly independent travel agents who work from home or visit customers. These independent travel agents often specialize in a certain type of trip, such as nature trips to Iceland or cycling holidays in Spain. Data from Statistics Netherlands (CBS) shows that the number of travel agencies registered with the Chamber of Commerce has increased by 32 percent over the past ten years and that this growth is mainly due to the number of self-employed people. At the same time, the number of travel agencies in retail properties has fallen by 52 percent over the past ten years, according to data from Locatus.

Artificial intelligence-based resources such as ChatGPT are also increasingly consulted by younger generations. For example, in the 18-24 age group, the use of AI increased by 11 percentage points between April 2024 and July this year. It is also clear that online travel agencies are losing popularity as a source of travel information among this generation. The rise of AI shopping agents, digital assistants that can independently book routes, accommodations and flights, is accelerating this trend. Travel marketer Propellic researched that the majority of AI searches lead to a booking, directly with the accommodation or through a travel agent. This puts pressure on online booking platforms such as Booking and Expedia.

## Choice stress and social media

Social media, such as Instagram and TikTok, make it easier to share photos and videos of travel with many people. This ensures that young people are more often exposed to the beautiful trips of others and that the demand for similar trips also grows. In order to make the right choice, younger generations more often seek help from a travel agent or agency, according to research by [De Volkskrant](https://www.volkskrant.nl/binnenland/het-fysieke-reisbureau-is-nog-altijd-in-trek-ook-bij-jongeren-het-is-vaak-een-geruststelling-voor-ouders~b085deea/). Travel agencies provide expert advice (often local partners), security (by joining ANVR, SGR) and practical assistance such as transport from the airport to their accommodation. This can relieve your worries and reduce the stress of choice.

## Effects on the travel industry

For the travel industry, the rise of AI and personal travel agents is a structural shift. Travelers are no longer just looking for the lowest price, but also for the ideal holiday for them. Travel agencies that are able to combine personal service with digital expertise therefore become valuable as a link of trust between AI and the traveler.

Travel organizations would do well to develop from traditional travel shops to service providers that efficiently plan a personalized trip with the help of AI. The role shifts from someone who not only sells, but guides and inspires during the search. The organizations that best combine human advice with the digital precision of the new technologies will be the winners of this technological disruption.
