# Shrinking livestock creates overcapacity in the food industry

The Dutch livestock herd is expected to shrink between 12 and 15 percent. The structural decline in the supply of milk and meat is creating overcapacity in the dairy and meat sector. Companies that are closer to the farmer, such as slaughterhouses, are hit harder than processors and traders further down the chain. They have more flexibility to adapt to a decreasing supply.

> “Shrinking livestock forces the dairy and meat sector to focus on more value instead of more volume”

Rob Morren

Sector Banker Food

## Supply of milk and meat is declining

The shrinkage of the Dutch livestock herd – as a result of cessation and skimming schemes – is disrupting the dairy and meat sector. The supply of meat and milk from Dutch soil is decreasing, which has the effect of pushing up prices. This is beneficial for many remaining farmers who see their incomes increase despite international price formation. Companies that purchase from the farmer are often faced with higher costs that can only be passed on to the consumer to a limited extent. A second consequence is that volumes decline, which threatens overcapacity at companies that, for example, slaughter animals or process milk. These companies will come under pressure if they do not make adjustments to their revenue model. Companies can anticipate in time by entering into a closer relationship with suppliers, sourcing supplies from abroad and focusing on added value instead of volume. A shrinking livestock herd also offers companies opportunities to become more sustainable. Fewer animals, higher added value, and the possibility of combining animal and vegetable protein in products.
