# Quality as a goal, AI as a means

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Transport & Logistics](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/logistiek/index.html)

05/09/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Bart Banning](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/bart-banning/index.html)

In the world of logistics, everything revolves around costs and efficiency. In times when transport costs will continue to rise, it is important to look at how logistics companies can improve productivity in the chains together with their customers. Using AI gives companies the opportunity to do this; not as an end in itself, but as a means.

It is not without reason that the theme of AI was central during the 25th edition of the Top 100 Logistics Service Providers earlier this year. Everyone is talking about AI, but how are the opportunities of AI seen from the perspective of companies in the logistics sector?

## AI increases the efficiency of logistics chains

The entries for the Top 100 Logistics Service Providers of 2025 show that AI is increasingly being used by these types of companies. As an innovation theme, AI fits very well in determining the ranking that a logistics company occupies on this list. It is increasingly about how companies are actually applying innovations in general, and AI in particular, in their daily operations.

The added value of AI can be great for companies that have gained the first serious experiences. It contributes to solving major challenges faced by the sector such as cost increases, emission reduction, labor market and lack of space. The challenge is to use the existing capacity of people and square meters in a smarter way. The digital infrastructure is one of the determining factors that guide the increasingly smarter design of logistics chains. AI is the next phase in the digital route, and is not a hype but a topic that will determine the progress of every logistics company in the coming years.

Please note that the way and extent to which AI is applied will differ per company. This is clear from the video series by Logistiek.nl in which a number of companies explained how they see the opportunities of AI. Major differences are visible there, which leads to interesting insights for everyone.

## Quality is the goal, AI the means

As a Top 100 jury member I was allowed to interview Nick Lenting. Nick is IT director of VTS Transport and Logistics in Boxmeer, which won the innovation award for the second year in a row in 2025. "VTS is absolutely working on all kinds of AI. We do this to support the organization, but also to develop new products for customers. VTS has been working on digitalization for years. It is really at the top of the agenda for the coming years."

> “We start with one process and expand from there”

Nick Lenting

IT Director at VST transport and Logistics

VTS wants to achieve optimizations with AI. Time savings for employees, for example, allowing them to focus more on quality. Quality is the goal, and AI the means. It is important to start small, with the low-hanging fruit. VTS starts with one process and expands from there. Another tip is to create support among employees. By this, VTS refers to the importance of everyone coming up with ideas for process improvements, after which they can look at the solution together.
