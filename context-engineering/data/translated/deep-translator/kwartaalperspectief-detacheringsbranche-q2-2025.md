# Quarterly perspective on the secondment industry Q2 2025

[Report](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/reports/index.html)

[Cross-sector](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/alle-sectoren/index.html)

13/08/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[Han Mesters](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/han-mesters/index.html)

Secondment workers continue to shrink. The turnover of the 59 secondments consulted within the VvDN fell by 8.2 percent in the second quarter of 2025, compared to the same period in 2024. Ten of the eleven reported end markets show a contraction in turnover compared to the second quarter of 2024. In the second quarter of 2025, there was also a significant contraction in total self-employed turnover, by 24.89 percent. year-on-year.

## Relevant trends that influence the results of secondment companies

Importantly, the analysis of the second quarter 2025 results begins with the Conference Board C-suite's 2025 geopolitical outlook: “Political and economic volatility are reshaping business strategy as sudden policy shifts, tax increases and regulatory changes disrupt business and investment decisions. Fueled by conflict-related disruptions and an increase in protectionist policies, the cost of global business operations has spiked over the past decade. Executives view geopolitical instability as one of the biggest threats to global growth. The traditional business strategy, which focused on market size, costs and efficiency, has been disrupted. Geopolitics is now the dominant factor influencing business decisions.

## Interpretation of the economy and persistent uncertainties

In the second quarter of 2025, government consumption was 2.8 percent higher compared to a year earlier. household consumption was 1.0 percent higher and investments grew by 2.5 percent. The increase in exports was 2.2 percent. However, the increase in imports was stronger, at 3.7 percent, causing the trade balance to contribute negatively to growth. The question is what the impact of geopolitical changes is on our exports in particular. From an economic perspective, many uncertainties remain, which makes projections difficult, including for this year.

## Explanation of the labor market: decrease in secondment turnover and fewer self-employed people

In the third quarter of 2024 we saw a stronger decline in turnover at secondment companies (VvDN quarterly monitor) than the development of GDP would suggest. The broadcast figures from the market monitor of trade association ABU show a stronger recovery in the first quarter of 2025 than the VvDN figures. Given the early cyclical nature of temporary employment, this is hopeful for secondment, if we only analyze secondment from a cyclical perspective.

Recent figures from Statistics Netherlands and market reports from VvDN also show that the number of self-employed people is decreasing. There could be a case of self-employed people switching to secondments, the so-called 'reverse waterbed effect'. However, we do not see this development reflected in the VvDN market monitor. The decline in the number of self-employed people is a logical development given the increasing enforcement of the DBA Act and the desire of organizations for more security and fewer hiring risks. A possible explanation for the fact that the number of self-employed people is decreasing but does not move on to secondments is that hirers more often choose to employ self-employed people themselves on a permanent basis.

## Decreasing number of full-time jobs and bench sitters (idle rate)

The VvDN market monitor for the second quarter of 2025 shows that the trend of a declining number of full-time jobs (FTEs) among seconded companies continues and that the idle rate remains reasonably stable. We have previously indicated reasons for turnover: many seconded people become permanent employees of the hirer.

## The candidate and the hirer: permanent employment is increasing

Given the many uncertainties currently in the economy, it is understandable that many employees prefer permanent employment. For both hirers and seconders, the percentages of employees with permanent employment appear to be steadily increasing (as part of the total number of employees). The scarcity of employees has been an important driver of this development in recent years. For many hirers, cost savings are also a reason to cut the costs of external hiring and even that work that used to be largely carried out by secondments is now increasingly carried out by their own employees. Examples include work on Know Your Customer and other compliance-related activities.

## Recommendations

The recruitment and selection function is crucial for secondments, especially in a labor market characterized by persistent scarcity. An important part of the added value that secondments can provide to their customers is the ability to supply the right candidates, especially in a scarce market. The added value of the recruitment and selection function is based on three things:

- Recruitment marketing (and AI)
- Managing expectations ('putting round pegs into square holes')
- 'Duty of care' to the candidate

## Other recommendations

Think carefully about which end markets to serve.

- Focus on scarce niche markets and profiles such as the secondment of psychiatrists, secondment of captains in inland shipping, secondment in the life sciences sector or secondment in the Dutch nuclear sector.
- Still opportunities in: IT, data, engineering, cybersecurity, energy transition.
- Less cyclical markets have higher rates.
