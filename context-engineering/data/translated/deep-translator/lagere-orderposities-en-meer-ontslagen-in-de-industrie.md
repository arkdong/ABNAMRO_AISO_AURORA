# Lower order positions and more layoffs in the industry

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

02/05/2025 • 2 minutes reading

[David Kemps](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-kemps/index.html)

Conditions for the Dutch industrial sector remained challenging in April with a continued decline in new orders and only marginal production growth. The Nevi Purchasing Managers Index (Nevi PMI) fell from 49.6 to 49.2 in April 2025, the lowest level in three months.

High costs of personnel, energy and raw materials, but especially weak demand due to Trump's capricious policy with high import duties, or not at all, ensure that manufacturers are cautious with their purchasing and investment decisions. Despite the fact that industrial companies produced slightly more in March and April, purchasing managers expect fewer new orders for the next three months. This applies across the board: from chemistry to metal, from mechanical engineering to semiconductor. For example, when publishing its first quarterly figures, ASML reported a 20 percent lower inflow of new orders than the average analysts had expected.

A trade war between the US and China could lead to structural changes in global trade flows, with major consequences for Dutch industry. Although only 6 percent of total Dutch exports are destined for the US, part of Dutch exports to Germany ultimately end up in products for the American market, such as cars and machines. ABN AMRO is therefore reducing its sector forecast for industry to 1 percent growth in 2025 (from 4 percent) and 4 percent in 2026. From 2026, Dutch industry is expected to benefit from the planned major investments in European defense and German infrastructure.

## More layoffs expected

The number of employees in Dutch factories fell for the ninth month in a row. The pace of job losses in April was the fastest since December 2023, mainly due to reductions in temporary staff and failure to replace those who leave. However, layoffs are mainly expected in the coming period. Benefit agency UWV reports a significant increase in the number of collective dismissals in the first quarter of 2025. The main reasons for the dismissals are cost control and reorganisations. This currently mainly affects employees at SME companies in the industry, but in addition to the closure of a number of large chemical factories in Rotterdam, Tata Steel Netherlands has also announced mass layoffs. These interventions are largely due to the high energy costs. However, when this text was written, it was not yet known whether the proposed climate and energy package with half a billion euros in government support had been included in the Spring Memorandum. This would be a major boost for energy-intensive industries such as the chemical and steel industries to remain in the Netherlands.

## Read more in the industrial sector

The industry is a heavyweight in the Dutch economy. Export is an important driver for this, but at the same time creates uncertainty. There are also themes that have a positive influence on growth, such as cooperation in the chain, innovation and sustainability.
