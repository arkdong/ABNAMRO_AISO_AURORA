# Made by Marí makes local craft scalable

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Retail](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/retail/index.html)

03/09/2025 • 5 minutes reading

[Henk Hofstede](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/henk-hofstede/index.html)

Marie Marie Amsterdam opts for local craft and connection. The crocheted bags, made by Dutch hobbyists, find their way to consumers at home and abroad. Together with the Made by Marí platform, nominated for the ABN AMRO Newcomer Retail Award 2025-2026, the founders also make local craftsmanship scalable for other brands.

The story of [Marie Marie Amsterdam](https://marie-amsterdam.com/) – founded in 2022 – starts with a bag that Marthe De Groot buys from her grandmother's crochet club. “I received so many compliments about it at home in Amsterdam,” she says. "Then I thought: what a shame that this kind of craft remains hidden. How can I bring these crocheters together with people who want to buy their work?"

## From hobby to community

During the corona period, De Groot decides to take the idea seriously. She places calls in Facebook groups and dozens of crocheters sign up in no time. Marie Marie Amsterdam now has a community of 200 crocheters, spread across hubs in the country. Each hub has a coordinator who distributes the materials and patterns. “For many women it is valuable to connect their creativity to something bigger,” says De Groot. "It shows that their handiwork has an impact, far beyond the hobby. All our bags have a handwritten card with the name of the maker.

Not only me, but also Joke, Corrie and Jolanda are very proud of our bags that are now available at the Bijenkorf.”

The colorful crocheted bags from Marie Marie Amsterdam are a hit. In addition to our own webshop and a store in De Pijp, the bags are now available at various international customers, such as The Iconic in Australia and Le Bon Marche in Paris. This spring the brand launched a collaboration with the Dutch brand Fabienne Chapot. There was also a promotion at Lowlands this year.

> “The colorful crocheted bags from Marie Marie Amsterdam are a hit.”

## Active in 60 WhatsApp groups

The popularity also brings challenges. “I was overwhelmed by the increase in crocheters and the demand from shops,” De Groot reflects. "The most difficult thing was to arrange everything operationally and make it scalable. I was in 60 WhatsApp groups and was involved in community management full-time with my mother."

At that moment Rinke Adema comes into the picture. With her experience at tech companies and startups, she immediately sees an opportunity. “I met Marthe and thought: you have to digitize this. With a platform that automates everything: from onboarding of makers to project management and payments.”

## Making craftsmanship scalable

The two soon decided to think bigger with their platform than just crocheted bags. “It has actually escalated a bit,” Adema laughs. "We saw that many makers have multiple hobbies: not only crocheting, but also ceramics, making furniture or clothing. In addition, Marthe noticed in her contacts with the fashion industry that many brands want to work with craft, but get stuck in their own models. That is why we started Made by Marí in 2024: a platform that brings makers and brands together and makes craftsmanship scalable."

> “You have to digitize this. With a platform that automates everything: from onboarding of makers to project management and payments.”

Rinke Adema

Founder Made by Marí

The founders also see their platform as an answer to growing pressure from legislation and regulations. Adema: "The government is increasingly demanding sustainable solutions, and many brands are struggling with this. With our platform we make it possible to produce small-scale and locally, exactly where the sector needs to go," says Adema.

## The dot on the horizon

Over the past year, the entrepreneurs worked on developing that platform. A first version is now available. "With Marie Marie Amsterdam as our first customer, we are now testing the system, removing bugs and learning how to do this properly in the long term. You start small, but you must already have a vision for five to ten years ahead," says Adema.

That dot on the horizon is bright. Adema: “We want to be a partner for large, international brands with an international community of makers. From Tokyo to Terneuzen. Imagine: a brand like Uniqlo that collaborates with local embroiderers in every city. Or key rings from Nike, made by local makers. I also believe in Made by Marí evenings with makers all over the world.”

De Groot adds: “We believe that this platform can shake up the fashion industry and inspire it to rethink production models. Back in time, but with a touch of the future. The fashion industry is now large, polluting and complicated. We want to bring it closer to people.”

## A change in mentality is desperately needed

Shaking up the industry is desperately needed according to the founders. "Some people find Marie Marie bags expensive. But if you consider how many hours, love and care go into them, it is actually dirt cheap. It really requires a change in consumer mentality. We believe in inspiring, rather than imposing." According to Adema, that change starts with the big brands. “If the brands that made fast fashion big now start recording small-scale, local collections, this will set in motion a huge movement.”

> “We believe this platform can shake up the fashion industry and inspire it to rethink production models.”

Marthe de Groot

Founder Made by Marí

For De Groot, the nomination for the ABN AMRO Newcomer Retail Award 2025–2026 feels like confirmation that they are on the right track with Marie Marie Amsterdam and the Made by Marí platform. “We haven't been around for very long, so it's special that we're being seen by people in the field.” Adema adds: “It is also the perfect stage to get in touch with other retailers who can use our platform.”

## The nicest and the toughest test

Finally, what do they want to pass on to other starting entrepreneurs? Adema: "Don't give up. You will always encounter hassle and setbacks, but perseverance wins. Keep believing in your vision. We are not yet making money with the platform, which is why I work alongside it full-time."

De Groot concludes: "You have to do something that you are passionate about, otherwise you won't last. Of course there are risks, but we are not afraid of them. There is always a way. This is the most fun, but also the toughest thing we have ever done."
