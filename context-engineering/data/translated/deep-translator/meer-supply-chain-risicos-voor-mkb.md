# More supply chain risks for SMEs

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cybersecurity](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/veilig/index.html)

29/12/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

Digital risks increasingly arise outside the own company. In November, the number of supply chain incidents increased again. Mainly due to vulnerabilities in software and the abuse of zero-day leaks.

Attackers target software that is used in thousands of organizations simultaneously. As a result, one weak spot in the chain can immediately have a major impact.

## Demonstrate NIS-2 conditions

More and more large companies are including NIS2 conditions in their purchasing policy. They require demonstrable cyber hygiene and clear patch management from suppliers. The demand for NIS2-QM certification is therefore increasing noticeably, especially among SMEs that want to meet the requirements of their chain partners. To collaborate with major customers, you must act now.

## Cybersecurity step-by-step plan

1. Gain insight into your software and dependencies. Map out which systems you use, which suppliers belong to them and how quickly vulnerabilities are resolved.
2. Check whether suppliers have demonstrably implemented basic measures. Asking about certification works fastest, but you can also ask about details such as patch policies, MFA, backup procedures and incident reporting. More and more organizations expect this as a minimum standard.
3. Record agreements and make them demonstrable. Agreements on digital security help manage risks and meet NIS2 expectations. Demonstrability via NIS2-QM has now become a decision factor for many customers.
