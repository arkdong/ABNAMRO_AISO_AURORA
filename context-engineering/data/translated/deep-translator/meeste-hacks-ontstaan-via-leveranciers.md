# Most hacks originate through suppliers

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cybersecurity](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/veilig/index.html)

12/05/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

Many entrepreneurs wonder: Why do I need to check my suppliers? It may feel uncomfortable and may affect the collaboration. Yet the figures show why this is so important.

Research by the Institute for Associations, Industries and Professions (IVBB) shows that most cyber attacks occur via suppliers and the supply chain:

1. Suppliers and supply chains – 40.1%
2. Own technology with vulnerabilities – 35.8%
3. Own employees (errors) – 24.1%

So many of the successful cyber attacks come through suppliers. That is why it is crucial to make clear agreements with them about cybersecurity.

## Which sectors are most attacked?

Some sectors are more attractive to cybercriminals than others. These are the top 10 sectors with the most cyber attacks:

1. Construction – 17%
2. Manufacturing – 14%
3. Healthcare – 14%
4. Retail – 12%
5. Technology – 11%
6. Transport – 7%
7.Legal – 7%
8. Education – 6%
9. Financial services – 6%
10. Automotive – 6%
11. Other sectors – 23%

Does your company fall on this list? Then it is extra important to arrange your cybersecurity properly and check your suppliers. They can be the weakest link.

## What can you do?

- Make clear agreements with suppliers about cybersecurity.
- Check regularly whether they adhere to these agreements.
- Assess risks and ensure backups and contingency plans.
