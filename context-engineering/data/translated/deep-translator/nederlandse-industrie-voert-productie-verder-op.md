# Dutch industry continues to increase production

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

01/09/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Albert Jan Swart](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/albert-jan-swart/index.html)

The Nevi Purchasing Managers Index for Dutch industry for August came to 51.9, the same score as the previous month. There are underlying shifts. The sub-index for production rose sharply, while that for new orders fell.

The number of new orders is still growing, but at a slower pace than in the previous month. The growth in the number of new export orders has virtually come to a standstill. This may be related to the American import duties, which have been set at 15 percent for many products. A rate of 50 percent even applies to steel and aluminum.

Fortunately, the European Commission and the White House announced on Thursday, August 22, that the 50 percent tariff on steel and aluminum will only apply from a certain quota. Nevertheless, the tariffs could put pressure on exports to the United States of steel and, for example, machinery. Exports to Germany of car parts, for example, are also likely to be under pressure. Chip machines and pharmaceutical products are currently excluded, but it is possible that President Donald Trump will introduce separate tariffs for these sectors. However, it has been agreed with the European Union that these rates will not exceed 15 percent.

The uncertain geopolitical situation and import duties are likely to depress investments. Nevertheless, the Nevi Purchasing Managers Index indicates growth in activity. Employment is also increasing at a significant pace, which has not occurred since the end of 2022.

## ASML saves costs

It is possible that activity will grow due to the growth of the chip market, although there are also other voices among ASML's suppliers. Some suppliers say they are experiencing a significant drop in orders from the chip machine manufacturer from Veldhoven. The demand for older machines in particular is disappointing, according to one of ASML's suppliers. ASML's latest machines probably benefit more from the growing demand for advanced chips for use in data centers for AI applications. Older chip machines, for example, are used to produce chips for the automotive industry, which is suffering from the trade war. The market could improve in the fourth quarter or next year, a purchasing manager said.

On the other hand, it is also not inconceivable that demand for older machines is better than expected, but that ASML limits its own inventories to increase profitability. In the past, it has often happened that suppliers complained about declining orders, and afterwards it turned out that ASML had delivered more machines to customers. Suppliers may also be affected by ASML's ambitious cost reduction targets in the coming years. Many suppliers are redesigning parts of ASML machines to produce them at significantly lower costs. Efforts are also being made to design the chip machines in a more modular way, so that parts of existing machines can be easily replaced.

## Defense industry growing under the radar

A number of companies have developed overcapacity, which is at least partly filled by investments in production capacity for the defense industry. This often concerns vacant factory halls in which new production lines are being set up. For example, VDL is building lines for defense in the old Nedcar factory in Born. Some high-tech companies are also investing. The machine industry and service providers involved in installation, among others, can benefit from this. So the defense industry is growing under the radar.

In the short term, the industry's recovery remains uncertain. President Trump's import duties are putting pressure on European exports, including in the chemical industry, for example. In addition, the chemical industry, for example, is suffering from increasing competition from China, which finds it more difficult to sell products on the American market. The uncertain geopolitical situation may also put pressure on investments. On the other hand, government investments in defense and infrastructure could stimulate the industry in the coming years.

## Read more in the industrial sector

The industry is a heavyweight in the Dutch economy. Export is an important driver for this, but at the same time creates uncertainty. There are also themes that have a positive influence on growth, such as cooperation in the chain, innovation and sustainability.
