# Dutch industry sees exports picking up

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

03/11/2025 • 2 minutes reading

[Albert Jan Swart](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/albert-jan-swart/index.html)

Dutch industry is growing at a slightly slower pace, as shown by the Nevi Purchasing Managers Index for October. The score dropped from 53.7 to 51.8. Production and the number of new orders increased further. The number of new export orders rose again, after a slight decline in September.

The recovery in exports is striking, because European exports to the US are under pressure due to American import duties. But the survey among approximately 350 purchasing managers in Dutch industry shows that European demand for Dutch products is increasing, especially from France, Germany and Belgium. However, many respondents answered before China restricted semiconductor exports, which could hinder the German auto industry in particular.

## Industrial policy important for basic industry

A division is becoming increasingly clear in the Dutch industry. On the one hand, production in the high-tech industry is starting to pick up, for example due to an improvement in the demand for machines. On the other hand, the energy-intensive industry is under pressure. For example, production in the chemical industry fell further during the summer, according to figures from the Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS). And in October, chemistry suffered a new setback. While several factories in the Rotterdam region announced their closure earlier this year, three chemical factories in Chemelot near Geleen in Limburg are now also closing their doors.

The election manifestos show that the various political parties are far from aligned on issues important to basic industry, such as the national CO2 tax and the size of budgets for sustainability subsidies, such as SDE++, EIA, MIA and VAMIL. The competitive position of the Dutch basic industry is under pressure, partly due to high energy prices and grid rates, which are significantly higher in the Netherlands than in neighboring countries.

Many factions in the House of Representatives are in favor of lower network rates, as became apparent during an election debate between nine candidate MPs that was organized by the trade association FME, research institute TNO and the trade unions. However, lowering network tariffs for industry, as the European Commission advises, is easier said than done, as this is likely to increase national debt.

The next cabinet can have a major influence on Dutch climate policy and the competitive position of the Dutch basic industry. The basic industry is therefore eagerly awaiting the formation of a new coalition.

## Read more in the industrial sector

The industry is a heavyweight in the Dutch economy. Export is an important driver for this, but at the same time creates uncertainty. There are also themes that have a positive influence on growth, such as cooperation in the chain, innovation and sustainability.
