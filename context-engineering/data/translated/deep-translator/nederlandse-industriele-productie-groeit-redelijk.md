# Dutch industrial production is growing reasonably well

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

01/12/2025 • 2 minutes reading

[Albert Jan Swart](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/albert-jan-swart/index.html)

The cautious recovery of Dutch industry continues, although the growth rate of production decreased slightly in November, according to the Nevi Purchasing Managers Index for Dutch industry.

The score of the Nevi Purchasing Managers Index is 51.8, equal to the score for the previous month. The number of new orders continued to grow at almost the same pace.

The demand for capital goods in particular is increasing, according to the survey among approximately 350 purchasing managers. The demand for machines is growing, probably because customers are continuing with their investment plans after earlier postponements. Many entrepreneurs postponed investment plans due to the trade restrictions introduced by President Trump. Now that the effects of this seem to be less than expected, the demand for machines is increasing again, such as machines for the agricultural sector.

## False start in chip market

The demand for machines for the chip industry is also growing. Yet a number of suppliers are in bad shape. For example, the dip in the chip market in recent years had created excess stocks at many ASML suppliers. It also happens that suppliers, anticipating growing demand from ASML, have produced additional parts too early, a false start. This is especially annoying if ASML is by far the company's largest customer. Some suppliers have significantly reduced production to reduce excess inventory. They are also cutting employment, outsourcing less work and looking for new sales markets.

## Nexperia effect isn't too bad

The effect of the shortage of Nexperia chips seems to be less than expected so far. Although delivery times increased considerably, the industry is used to something. In addition, demand is not growing at a high pace, so the need for parts is not too bad.

What is more annoying is that the costs of industrial companies are rising. Wages, material prices and energy are becoming more expensive, many respondents indicate.

For the second month in a row, employment fell slightly, partly due to an improvement in productivity. This higher labor productivity will be desperately needed if the industry starts to grow faster again in 2026 and the personnel shortage increases again.

## Read more in the industrial sector

The industry is a heavyweight in the Dutch economy. Export is an important driver for this, but at the same time creates uncertainty. There are also themes that have a positive influence on growth, such as cooperation in the chain, innovation and sustainability.
