# Dutch superyachts remain popular

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

15/08/2025 • 6 minutes reading

[Martijn Mensonides](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/martijn-mensonides/index.html)

At the end of August, Amsterdam will host SAIL, the largest nautical event in the Netherlands. The craftsmanship that made the Netherlands great in shipbuilding is still visible and forms the basis for the current Dutch superyacht industry, which is at the top worldwide. After record years, the market is showing signs of cooling down. New opportunities present themselves to American tech billionaires, who seek adventure in addition to luxury. The US import duties are expected to cause minimal disruption to the market.

## Audience of millions for top maritime event

This year, Amsterdam celebrates its 750th anniversary with the 10th edition of SAIL Amsterdam. Due to the corona pandemic, the previous edition could not take place, so the anniversary has been moved to 2025. SAIL Amsterdam has grown into the largest freely accessible maritime event in the world. This year's event is expected to attract 2.3 million visitors from home and abroad and more than 10,000 ships, 800 of which are part of the official parade. Among national, freely accessible events, SAIL Amsterdam takes the undisputed first place in terms of visitor numbers, followed by the Nijmegen Four Days Marches with 1.5 million visitors.

Tall ships and historic ships in particular attract attention. They demonstrate the craftsmanship that has distinguished the Netherlands for centuries and is still visible today in the construction of superyachts. A niche in which the Netherlands has a top position worldwide. Although no official definition exists, a yacht over 24 meters is usually considered a superyacht. These are extremely expensive, custom-made ships with full crew.

## The Netherlands excels in exclusive long yachts

The Netherlands maintains its top position not so much through production volumes, but through the length and quality of the yachts it produces. In 2024, the Netherlands was the world's largest producer of new superyachts longer than 80 meters. The reason why clients choose Dutch yacht builders is because of their specialization in customization, innovation and craftsmanship. [Almost 70 percent](https://cdn.boatinternational.com/files/2024/12/8ec72830-b163-11ef-a6c6-491cb76a0430-GOB_2025_upload.pdf) of Dutch production consists of custom work: ships that are designed and built completely from scratch according to the wishes of a client. Well-known Dutch superyacht yards are Feadship, Oceanco, Heesen Yachts and Royal Huisman.

Germany also holds the record with the [Azzam](https://www.lurssen.com/en/new-build/yachts/azzam/), the largest superyacht ever with a length of almost 181 meters, built by the German Lürssen Yachts. German builders mainly focus on very large boats: the average length of German superyachts will be 99.2 meters in 2025. For comparison: the average length of superyachts from Dutch builders, who serve a broader segment, is according to the magazine [BOAT International](https://cdn.boatinternational.com/files/2024/12/8ec72830-b163-11ef-a6c6-491cb76a0430-GOB_2025_upload.pdf) 71 meters.

## Inflation drives up meter prices

The former rule of thumb of 1 million euros per meter for a superyacht is now around 1.3 million euros per meter. Extra length and luxury often drive the final meter price up further. (Source: HISWA-RECRON)

The yacht segment between 30 and 60 meters is dominated by Italian companies, which were responsible for two-thirds of sales in 2024. Italian builders mainly focus on increasing volume and producing in series. In 2025, the average length of Italian superyachts will be 55.4 meters.

## Economic climate superyacht market

According to calculations by ABN AMRO and HISWA-RECRON, the business organization for companies in the yacht building and water sports industry, the global market share of Dutch superyachts will amount to approximately a quarter in 2023. According to the latest figures, approximately 6,500 employees work in the industry in the Netherlands, of which approximately 3,000 work for superyacht builders themselves and approximately 3,500 work for suppliers. Turnover in 2023 has fallen by 8.5 percent compared to 2022. What is striking is a 48 percent increase in the export value in 2023. This is probably due to the increased orders for superyachts during the [corona pandemic](https://maritiemland.nl/maritieme-monitor-2024/). The construction of a superyacht takes a few years, so delivery takes place later.

Global sales of superyachts have also declined in recent years. It seems that the market has cooled down after the record years and is returning to normal. The number of delivered superyachts has been temporarily reduced by sanctions against Russian buyers, following the Russian invasion of Ukraine in February 2022. Due to the sanctions, superyachts already ordered by sanctioned Russians were no longer allowed to be delivered. These superyachts, often with adjustments to the interior, for example, were ultimately delivered with delay to buyers from other countries.

In recent years, more and more shipyards have started building superyachts without having yet found a buyer. This so-called 'building on speculation' makes it easier to respond to customers who immediately want a superyacht and ensure a more stable use of production capacity. In 2023, the share was 19 percent, and by 2025 this will have increased to 31 percent. The share is especially larger for smaller superyachts (ships between 24 and 27 meters), namely 40 percent. In the Netherlands, more than a quarter of superyacht construction is currently speculative.

## Superyachts for the new explorers

The market is shifting from traditional billionaires to young, technology-driven customers. They want less standard luxury and more adventure. So-called expedition yachts are therefore becoming increasingly popular: more compact yachts with a larger and robust design, suitable for remote areas such as Antarctica or the Southern Ocean. [Autonomy](https://www-onboard-mc.translate.goog/blog/traditional-vs-explorere-yacht?_x_tr_sl=en&_x_tr_tl=nl&_x_tr_hl=nl&_x_tr_pto=sge&_x_tr_hist=true) plays an important role in this, being able to stay on the water independently for a long time under challenging circumstances. An example of this is the [Xplorer 60](https://www.xploreryachting.com/portfolios/xplorer-60?gad_source=1&gad_campaignid=21727703485&gbr aid=0AAACoek61-kpJZOQR5WbrpoxLsBzMci&gclid=EAIaIQobChMI08Tnno7QjgMVH5WDBx1EmTF0EAAYASAAEgJY0PD_BwE), built by Damen shipyard. This 60-meter superyacht has a hybrid propulsion and has a so-called ice class certification. Over the past ten years, the number of expedition yachts has almost doubled, from 55 to 110 yachts.

## Ownership of superyacht builders is shifting

Dutch superyacht construction is mainly supported by family businesses. Due to rapid market growth and the desire of parties to move along, the capital requirement is increasing. Private equity parties and other external financiers are therefore increasingly participating. Examples include Royal Huisman Group, Vanquish Yachts and Steeler Yachts, which are currently partly owned by private equity. Italy, on the other hand, has a number of parties that are listed on the stock exchange, including Ferretti, Azimut, Sanlorenzo, Fincantieri and Italian Sea Group. In the Italian market, the emphasis is more on series production of smaller superyachts. Because the projects are smaller and require less customization, business operations are more consistent and predictable, making the companies more suitable for a stock exchange listing.

## Export expectations

Industry representatives expect that US President Donald Trump's import duties will have minimal impact on Dutch yacht builders. Although [24 percent](https://www.monacoyachtshow.com/cache/file/311793/monaco-yacht-show-market-report-2024.pdf) of yachts over 40 meters are owned by Americans, the flag state – the country in which the ship is registered – can easily be changed. Popular flag states include the Cayman Islands, the Marshall Islands, the United Kingdom and Malta. It is quite common for superyachts to sail under the flag of one of these countries.

Precise export data on specific superyachts is lacking. As an indication, according to figures from Statistics Netherlands (CBS), the total export value of pleasure and sports boats amounted to 3.6 billion euros in 2024. The majority of this export value concerns superyachts, but the product group also includes smaller yachts and sloops, for example. Most of the exports went to the Cayman Islands, Marshall Islands and the United Kingdom. Only 3 percent of exports were destined for the United States.
