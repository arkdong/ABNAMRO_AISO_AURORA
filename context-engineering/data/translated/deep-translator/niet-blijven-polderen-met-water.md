# Do not continue to polder with water

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

01/12/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Leontien de Waal](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/leontien-de-waal/index.html)

We have not yet finished solving congestion on the electricity grid and the next traffic jam is already emerging: drinking water congestion. It feels surreal in a country that averages 800 millimeters of rain per year, but scarcity is rarely about how much there is in total.

It's about availability, time, location, quality, demand patterns and our design choices. And let's be honest: we still design as if drinking water is an eternal self-evident fact.

## Focus on distinctiveness

The average consumption is about 128 liters per person per day and 80 percent of all drinking water is used by households. This shows that the challenge lies mainly in smarter design of water systems, in behavior and in temptation. The price doesn't help: drinking water is so cheap in the Netherlands that waste hardly hurts. This means there is no incentive to consume differently. As a developer you can wait for regulation or pricing policy, but you can also convert it into a distinctive character now.

> “The challenge for combating water congestion lies mainly in smarter design of water systems, in behavior and in temptation.”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction

## Circular water systems as standard

We must no longer build as if every household is an island. Learn from the lessons that we gradually apply to energy: storage, buffering, exchange and hubs. Rainwater that now falls free and abundantly on our roofs runs through the gutter into the sewer and then returns purified as expensive drinking water. That's not luxury, that's inefficiency in disguise.

There are opportunities for municipalities, developers and urban planners to approach new residential areas as circular water systems. Homes that retain, share, reuse and buffer water will become the new norm and will translate into value. This is the time to develop in anticipation. This also gives water companies leeway in the timing of their investment programs.

## Rate of expansion of pressurized treatment plants

After all, they still have to make much-needed expansion investments in treatment plants, because our freshwater is becoming saltier and has more pollution. At the same time, the profit that water companies are allowed to make is legally capped. In the short term, this is pleasant for households that have low drinking water costs, but this is a limitation that immediately limits the scope for investment.

Let us not continue to discuss and argue about congestion, but build area-specific, scarcity-intelligent neighborhoods, ensure that smart treatment plants are given priority over the overcrowded power grid and ensure that the investment space for water companies is structurally in order.

> “An area-specific approach to water scarcity is a necessity.”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction
