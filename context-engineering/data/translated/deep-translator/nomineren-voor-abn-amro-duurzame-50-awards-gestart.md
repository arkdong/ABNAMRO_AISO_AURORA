# Nominations for ABN AMRO Sustainable 50 Awards started

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Construction](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/bouw/index.html)

17/09/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Leontien de Waal](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/leontien-de-waal/index.html)

The 13th edition of the ABN AMRO Sustainable 50 has just started. The search has begun and until October 1, people can be nominated who will make a major impact in making the built environment more sustainable. The spotlight is on individual efforts that make a collective difference.

Nominations for the ABN AMRO Sustainable 50 awards are open. The ABN AMRO Sustainable 50 is the list of professionals who demonstrably contribute to a more sustainable built environment. They are the drivers, connectors, accelerators and innovators in the sector. Every year we put these people on stage to honor their work, commitment and vision and to inspire others to also get moving. The awards will be presented on November 5, during the Climate Summit for the Built Environment.

## Thinkers, makers and doers

Just like last year, companies active in the built environment are invited to nominate colleagues, partners or clients who manifest themselves as leaders. Who is making progress, who sets the agenda, works together and brings creativity and innovation? Who are those people who have the decisiveness, charisma, contacts, knowledge and vision to really make the built environment more sustainable? There are three categories in which nominations can be made:

1. The Creator: makes policy, designs, develops, advises and guides.
2. The Maker: produces and delivers sustainable concepts and products.
3. De Doener: builds, installs and assembles with an eye for circularity, energy efficiency and future value.

The overall winner of the Sustainable 50 comes from one of the above categories and wins both parts. In addition, the Young Professional Award will be presented again this year.

## From nominating to voting

Led by chairman Andy van den Dobbelsteen, professor of Climate Design & Sustainability and overall winner in 2024, the 12-member professional jury will assess the nominees. The professional jury awards points to each nominee based on [4 criteria](https://www.duurzaamgebouwd.nl/duurzaam-50). The jury then compiles a top 50 ranking and the public election will open from October 17. The awards will be presented at the [Climate Summit GO](http://www.climatetopgo.nl/) where all frontrunners will be welcomed at a festive ceremony on November 5, 2025.

## Broad jury composition

The jury also hopes to welcome a nice variety of nominees this year. Whether someone works as a self-employed person, at an innovative startup or at a large company, it is about demonstrable decisiveness and drive to make the built environment more sustainable. To properly weigh diversity, the size and composition of the jury is relevant. The jury represents the entire chain, from client to developer and supplier. Naturally, all jury members bring an affinity with sustainability into their 'package'.

> “We have a jury that oversees the full breadth of the construction and real estate chain and has an eye for the various roles.”

Leontien de Waal

Sector Banker Construction and jury member

The Dutch construction sector has been one of the best performing sectors in recent years, despite several challenges facing the sector. Examples are the nitrogen dossier, the significant price increases for energy and building materials and also the personnel shortages. Driven by trends, innovations and legislation and regulations, the relevance of themes such as sustainability and environmental impact is growing and the sector is looking for a new balance.
