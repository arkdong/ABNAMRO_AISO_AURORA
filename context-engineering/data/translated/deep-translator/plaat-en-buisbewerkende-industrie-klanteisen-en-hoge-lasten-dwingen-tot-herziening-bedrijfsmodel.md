# Sheet and tube processing industry: customer demands and high burdens force a business model to be revised

[Report](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/reports/index.html)

[Industry](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/industrie/index.html)

07/18/2025 • 2 minutes reading

[David Kemps](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-kemps/index.html)

Discussions with the sector and new figures show that more than 1,700 sheet and tube processors are under high pressure due to declining demand, rising management costs, high customer demands and staff shortages. Entrepreneurs face strategic choices to invest in automation and digital transformation to increase labor productivity and develop new sales markets.

The Dutch sheet and tube processing sector - with 1,700 to 2,000 companies spread across the country - faces major challenges. The net operating income of these companies amounted to 7.8 billion euros in 2023. However, little growth is expected in the coming years. Various round table discussions that ABN AMRO has held with entrepreneurs in the sector show that they are confronted with rising costs of labour, energy and materials, while labor productivity is declining. New figures point to an increasing number of bankruptcies and business closures in the sector. Traditional sales markets, such as mechanical engineering, the automotive industry and construction, are under pressure from global issues, such as the energy transition and geopolitical developments. This has led to a decline in demand for sheet metal and pipes, forcing companies to explore new markets such as defense.

## Three tasks and three solutions

To remain future-proof, sheet and tube processors must make strategic choices. In collaboration with the trade associations NEVAT and FPT, the report identifies three major tasks: increasing labor productivity, developing new sales markets and professionalization of management and possible succession. Automation and digital transformation, scaling and diversification and business model innovation are crucial to address these challenges.

> “If this innovation takes too long, many large customers will consider carrying out sheet and tube processing activities in-house”

David Kemps

Sector Banker Industry

## Business Model Canvas as a talking point

In addition, the market demands complete customer care and specialist knowledge. Companies must invest in sustainability, energy-efficient production machines and new staff skills. Market consolidation is inevitable, especially as many medium and small business owners approach retirement age. The Business Model Canvas, as described in the report, provides a structured approach for sheet and tube fabricators to analyze and refine their business models. This model can serve as a 'talking board' to inform stakeholders and support strategic adjustments.
