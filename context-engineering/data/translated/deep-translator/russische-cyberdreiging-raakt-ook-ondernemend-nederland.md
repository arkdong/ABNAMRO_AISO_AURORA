# Russian cyber threat also affects enterprising Netherlands

[Knowledge article](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/actueel.html)

[Cyber attack](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/cyberattack/index.html)

06/30/2025 • 2 minutes reading

ABN AMRO Editorial

Since 2024, a new name has emerged in the cyber espionage landscape: Laundry Bear. The AIVD and MIVD discovered this Russian cyber actor. Laundry Bear collects sensitive information from European institutions and companies, including in the Netherlands.

In the first known attack, employee business contact information was stolen. Since then, several organizations have been targeted by this sophisticated but subtle group.

## Living off the Land Methods

They use techniques such as [phishing](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/cybersecurity/wat-is-phishing.html), password spraying and so-called Living off the Land methods. Existing software in an organization is abused without the immediate need for malicious software, which makes detection difficult. The group also uses stolen authentication tokens, such as cookies, obtained through criminal marketplaces.

## What can you do?

To protect your organization you can, among other things:

- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA).
- Monitor systems for suspicious activities.
- Working with a zero-trust architecture.
- Conduct regular security audits.
- Better secure email traffic and train employees to recognize phishing.
- The threat from these types of groups is never over. Vigilance, up-to-date security and employee awareness are crucial.
