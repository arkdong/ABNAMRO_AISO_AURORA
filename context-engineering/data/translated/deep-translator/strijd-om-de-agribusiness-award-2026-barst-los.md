# Battle for the 2026 Agribusiness Award is on

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Agricultural](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/agrarisch/index.html)

17/09/2025 • 3 minutes reading

[Pierre Berntsen](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/autoriteit/pierre-berntsen/index.html)

Melanie Murk

Registration for the Agribusiness Award opened on Thursday, September 11. Companies in the agricultural and food chain with an innovative product, service or chain concept can register. An expert jury, led by the new jury chairman Professor Laurens Sloot, will assess all entries.

On February 10, 2026 - during the ABN AMRO Open in Rotterdam Ahoy - the prize will be presented and the successor to Royal Koopmans, the 2025 winner with the Nederwheat concept, will be announced.

The Agribusiness Award will be presented for the seventh time in 2026. The prestigious award is intended to stimulate and spotlight innovations in the agricultural and food sector. We look for innovations that help the sector move forward and have demonstrable added value for farmers and gardeners. With the Award, initiators ABN AMRO and DCA Market Intelligence want to express their appreciation for the innovative power of companies in the Dutch food and agribusiness.

## Smart and sustainable innovation with great market potential

These companies are cordially invited to register for the Agribusiness Award with their innovative product, service or chain concept. You can register until December 1, 2025 via the [website](https://agribusinessaward.nl/inschrijven). Any company can register, but to be eligible for the Award, a number of [conditions] apply (https://agribusinessaward.nl/regulations). This must be a smart and sustainable innovation that has already proven itself in practice. The innovation must also have great market potential and therefore not be tailor-made for one or a few farmers or gardeners.

## New jury composition

A four-member jury will assess all applications. This year it will be led by the new jury chairman Laurens Sloot, director at EFMI Business School and professor of Retail & Entrepreneurship at the University of Groningen. He succeeds Aalt Dijkhuizen, who remains closely involved as a jury member. In addition, Jan Opschoor, director of Sustainability and Business Development at Van Iperen, will join the jury as successor to Jan Omvlee (Rijk Zwaan). Pierre Berntsen, Director of ABN AMRO Agricultural Companies, is the fourth jury member.

> “This means we have a jury that oversees the full breadth of agriculture and horticulture, technology and sales chains.”

Pierre Berntsen

Director of ABN AMRO Agricultural Companies and fourth jury member

## Amount of money and media package

The jury will choose three nominees from all entries for the Agribusiness Award 2026. These three companies will be invited to the pitch day to present their innovation to the jury. All three companies will be given the stage during the ABN AMRO Open in Ahoy on February 10, 2026. The winner will be announced festively there. The winner will receive a cash prize of €5,000 and a media package worth €3,500. Last year, that honor went to Royal Koopmans with its Nedertarwe chain concept, which stands for bread made from sustainably grown wheat from the Netherlands. The jury also hopes to judge a wide variety of entries this year. “Innovations, large or small, don't matter, as long as they help the sector move forward.”
