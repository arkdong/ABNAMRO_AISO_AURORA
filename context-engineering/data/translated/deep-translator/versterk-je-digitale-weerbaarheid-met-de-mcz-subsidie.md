ABN AMRO Editorial

As an entrepreneur you want to move forward. Serving new customers, streamlining your processes, strengthening your team. But in this digital age there is an invisible danger lurking: cyber attacks. They increasingly affect SMEs and can not only paralyze your systems, but also damage your reputation. Fortunately, there is now a way to better protect your company against these risks – with the help of the MCZ subsidy.

## Why cybersecurity deserves priority

Cybercrime is no longer something that only affects large companies. Small and medium-sized enterprises in particular are increasingly becoming targets. Why? Because they are often less secure, even though they manage valuable data and resources. A cyber attack can easily cause tens of thousands of euros in damage and often brings your business operations to a standstill.

Investing in cybersecurity pays off. It not only prevents damage, but also gives your customers and employees confidence in the safety of your business environment.

## This is how the MCZ subsidy helps your company

The government offers financial support with [the MCZ subsidy](https://www.rvo.nl/subsidies-financing/mcz). With this scheme you receive a 50% discount on cybersecurity investments, up to a maximum of €1,250. This allows you to take a maximum of €2,500 in security measures. An ideal step to strengthen your digital security.

This subsidy is specifically intended for new investments, such as:

- securing your network and WiFi;
- setting up two-factor authentication;
- implementing backup systems;
- offering cyber awareness training for your team.

## Getting started with the MCZ subsidy in 5 steps

#### Step 1: Check if you qualify

Your company must meet three conditions. Maximum 50 employees, less than € 10 million annual turnover and a Chamber of Commerce registration.

#### Step 2: Complete the CyberSafe Check

The [CyberSafe Check](https://tools.digitaltrustcenter.nl/cyberveilig-check/) from the Digital Trust Center is mandatory. Complete this for the most relevant investments and download the advice as a PDF, without this document your application will be rejected.

#### Step 3: Invest smartly in your digital security

Now you choose which measures you take. You do not have to immediately follow all the advice from the CyberSafe Check, but it is a good guideline. Our cybersecurity experts are happy to help you with this.

#### Step 4: Save your supporting documents

Make sure your invoice contains all required information and preferably pay by bank for a direct bank statement.

#### Step 5: Submit your application

Registrations have been open since July 18, 2025. You can submit your application via RVO with your eHerkenning (minimum level 2+). The deadline is October 31, 2025.
