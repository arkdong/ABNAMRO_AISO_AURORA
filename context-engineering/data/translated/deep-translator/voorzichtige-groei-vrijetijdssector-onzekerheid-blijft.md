# Cautious growth in leisure sector, uncertainty remains

[Sector forecast](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/sectorprognoses/index.html)

[Leisure](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/leisure/index.html)

05/03/2026 • 3 minutes reading

[Ward van der Stee](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/ward-van-der-stee/index.html)

The Leisure sector has managed to overcome a number of hurdles in the past year, but the uncertainty continues this year as well. Hotels and holiday parks are confronted with an increase in VAT on overnight stays, which puts pressure on volumes. Yet there are also positive aspects: tourism continues to grow slightly and purchasing power appears to be improving slightly, which offers opportunities for the sector.

For 2026, the Dutch Tourism and Conventions Board (NBTC) expects a moderate growth in the number of guests of 0.5 percent. The growth in the number of foreign guests halves compared to previous years, while the number of Dutch guests decreases slightly. The slowdown in growth is mainly attributed to the VAT increase.

The coalition agreement of the new cabinet contains plans that will ensure that purchasing power will increase less rapidly this year than previously expected. Calculations by the Central Planning Bureau (CPB) show that purchasing power will increase by 0.2 percent. Since this government still has to find majorities in the House of Representatives for all plans, this calculation of the coalition agreement is surrounded by more uncertainties than previous calculations. The recently erupted conflict in Iran may also have an effect on purchasing power through increased energy prices.

The savings rate has been high in recent years: Dutch people saved a relatively large share of their disposable income. Dutch people therefore have a lot of savings. Together with the increased consumer confidence compared to a year ago, the expectation is that Dutch people will consume more: 1.3 percent according to the CPB. Although these extra expenses are obviously not only spent on leisure time, we do expect that the Leisure sector can benefit from this.

## VAT increase affects part of the sector

However, the unrest is not completely over in the sector. Since January 1, the VAT rate on accommodation has increased from 9 to 21 percent. The effects of this are still uncertain. For example, the question is to what extent accommodation accommodations pass on the VAT increase, whether and how many domestic and Dutch tourists book fewer overnight stays in the event of a price increase and whether they more often choose foreign alternatives. This makes it difficult again this year to estimate the growth in volume in the subsectors affected by this.

## Debt burden and passing on costs

In addition to uncertainty about the consequences of the VAT increase for part of the Leisure sector, a large number of entrepreneurs have a large debt burden. This was often built up during the corona pandemic and could only be repaid sparingly in the past year due to rising costs and therefore shrinking margins. Last November, one in five catering entrepreneurs indicated in the business survey by the Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS) that their debts are problematic. A larger proportion indicated that this debt had increased in the past year than the proportion where the debt had decreased; 7.4 percent versus 5.7 percent. In the culture, sport and recreation subsectors, one in eight companies has problematic debts.

The economic survey of January this year shows that debts are difficult to reduce. Approximately 58 percent of entrepreneurs in the catering industry indicate that they can only partially or not at all pass on the increased costs. Margins are therefore under pressure, leaving less room to repay debts.

## Read more in the Leisure sector

The Leisure sector has largely gone through a difficult period. The recovery has started with the phasing out of the measures regarding corona. The terraces are starting to fill up, the number of tourists is increasing and events can be organized again. Staff shortages are now the biggest challenge. Digitizing staff work and spreading guests can limit the impact.
