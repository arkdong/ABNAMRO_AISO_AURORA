# Employers are becoming increasingly better at finding newcomers

[Analysis](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/analyses/index.html)

[Cross-sector](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/alle-sectoren/index.html)

11/07/2025 • 8 minutes reading

[Mario Bersem](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/mario-bersem/index.html)

[Nehreen Al-Mahmoedi](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/nehreen-al-mahmoedi/index.html)

People who are new to the Netherlands, because they have fled their country, find a job faster and more often. Employers are increasingly finding these 'newcomers' in their search for talent. According to ABN AMRO, the number of working refugees will increase by 17,500 in 2025, an increase of 20 percent compared to 2024. This is mainly due to the urgent need for personnel and due to policy and regulations.

Newcomers are people who have applied for protection as refugees, 'asylum seekers'; people who have received protection as refugees, 'status holders'; and Ukrainian refugees who receive protection in the Netherlands under the European Temporary Protection Directive (RTB, 2001/55 EC). An estimated 88,000 newcomers were employed at the end of 2024, and ABN AMRO expects an increase of 17,500 employees or 20 percent by 2025.

Staff shortages ensure that refugees find work more often, because employers in a tight labor market are more likely to give new talent a chance. Tension on the labor market is high, with more vacancies than unemployed. Almost all sectors are faced with staff shortages, especially in sectors with many entry-level jobs such as the temporary employment sector, catering, cleaning and landscaping. Newcomers can help to alleviate pressure on the labor market, as examples from employers in this report show.

Policy and regulations have a significant impact on the labor participation of newcomers. This is evident, for example, from the sharp increase in the number of working asylum seekers since the so-called '24-week requirement' was abolished. The importance of regulations is also visible in the experience of Ukrainian refugees in the Netherlands. Their high labor participation rate, around 60 percent, shows what is possible if newcomers are given free access to the labor market.

Various companies know how to fill their vacancies by working with newcomers. Employment agencies and other HR service providers connect refugees to employers; they guide employers and candidates in entering into an employment relationship. Heroyam, for example, is an employment agency that places newcomers. Untapped Talents helps companies to hire candidates from asylum seeker centers (azcs).

## Ukrainian newcomers are finding their way into the labor market

Ukrainian refugees fall under the European Temporary Protection Directive and receive shelter, medical care and education for minor children in the Netherlands. Ukrainians have free access to the labor market. On November 1, 2024, 117,000 Ukrainians were in the Netherlands, of whom approximately 92,000 were aged 15-65.

Ukrainian newcomers relatively often have paid work. Nearly six in ten Ukrainians aged 15-65, approximately 55,000 people, were employed on November 1, 2024. This is evident from figures from the Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS). ABN AMRO expects the number of employed Ukrainians to rise further, to 65,000 by the end of 2025. That is an increase of 18 percent, or 10,000 employed people, compared to the end of 2024 (see graph).

The increase in the number of employed Ukrainians has three causes: the labor participation of Ukrainians increases the longer they stay in the Netherlands; the number of Ukrainians in the Netherlands is increasing; and the share of Ukrainians of working age – 15-65 years – is increasing because the population is relatively young, meaning that more young people are joining than older people are dropping out.

Ukrainians often work in the catering industry, agriculture and landscaping, but also in retail, cleaning and the transport and logistics sector, figures from the Employee Insurance Agency (UWV) show. More than half work full-time (53 percent) and often through employment agencies (38 percent). The temporary employment sector plays an important role in the integration of Ukrainians and other newcomers by offering accessible work, acting as an employer and facilitating advancement.

The experience of Ukrainian refugees in the Netherlands shows that the labor participation of newcomers can be high with free access to the labor market: around 60 percent and rising. In this analysis, ABN AMRO uses the labor participation of Ukrainians as a reference point to estimate the labor potential of other newcomers, a method that is also used, for example, in the report Wider work opportunities for asylum seekers from the Economic Research Foundation (SEO).

## Newcomers in the asylum phase more often work after a court ruling

Asylum seekers have applied for protection as refugees and usually stay in asylum seekers' centers while they go through their asylum procedure. These newcomers in the asylum phase are not simply allowed to work in the Netherlands. Yet the labor participation of asylum seekers is increasing sharply, especially since an important obstacle has been removed.

Asylum seekers could previously not work longer than 24 weeks in a year. After the Council of State (RvS) abolished this so-called '24-week requirement' in November 2023, the number of asylum seekers working has increased sharply: from approximately 2,500 employees in 2023 to approximately 8,000 in 2024, an increase of more than 200 percent, according to data provided by the UWV at the request of ABN AMRO (see graph). For 2025, ABN AMRO expects a further net increase of 25 percent or 2,000 active asylum seekers.

Asylum seekers still have the restriction that they are only allowed to work once they have been in the asylum procedure for six months, which usually starts seven weeks after arrival in the Netherlands. In addition, the employer must apply for a work permit (twv). If these restrictions were to disappear, the labor potential of these newcomers in the asylum phase could be reached more quickly and fully. This produces additional prosperity, as shown, for example, in the aforementioned SEO report.

The total labor potential of asylum seekers consists of around 21,000 people, ABN AMRO estimates. If asylum seekers had free access to the labor market, such as Ukrainian refugees, this total labor potential would come into view. The estimate is based on the total of 55,000 asylum seekers present in the Netherlands, the figure of the Central Agency for the Reception of Asylum Seekers (COA) in June 2025. Further assumptions: a 75 percent share of 15-65 year olds, estimated on the basis of COA data; and a potential labor participation rate of 50 percent, estimated based on the experience of Ukrainian refugees who have been in the Netherlands for a short time. This calculation serves to illustrate the unused labor potential among asylum seekers.

This large untapped labor potential offers opportunities for employers to find talent among asylum seekers. Organizations such as Untapped Talents play a crucial role by helping companies find and guide asylum seekers. The COA also helps residents of asylum seekers' centers through so-called 'Participation desks'. These are central places in the asylum center where residents can go for various activities and (volunteer) work. This approach not only provides a solution to staff shortages, but also promotes diversity and inclusivity. This allows companies to increase their social impact, which is often a requirement for government assignments.

## Newcomers with refugee status combine work and integration

Status holders are people who have received protection as refugees in the Netherlands. They have been given a temporary residence permit for usually five years and free access to the labor market. Status holders follow a mandatory integration process to get to know the Dutch language and culture. Status holders are more likely to combine work and integration.

In the short term, the labor participation of status holders may be hampered by the intensive integration courses, which usually take place during the day. Nevertheless, the labor participation of status holders is increasing significantly and is expected to continue to rise. The updated Integration Act of 2021 plays a role in increasing labor participation because it requires more participation during integration; this benefits employment. Because asylum seekers work more often, the labor participation of status holders will also receive an extra boost. The expectation is that working asylum seekers will continue to work when they receive status.

Figures from Statistics Netherlands show that recent cohorts of status holders find work more quickly compared to their predecessors (see graph). For example, of the status holders who received their status in 2020, 31 percent had a job after three years; of the 2018 group, this was still 21 percent after three years. For all cohorts, their labor participation increases the longer they stay in the Netherlands. For example, Cohort 2022 started with a 7.3 percent labor participation rate, which was already 12.2 percent a year later. The expectation is that the labor participation of status holders will continue to increase, per year and per cohort.

Based on CBS data for the 2019 to 2024 cohorts, ABN AMRO estimates the number of status holders in the 15-65 age category at 87,000 by the end of 2024. The estimated labor participation starts at 10 percent for the 2024 cohort and amounts to 44 percent after five years for the 2019 cohort. The number of working status holders at the end of 2024 was estimated at 25,000; ABN AMRO expects an additional 5,500 newcomers with refugee status to work in 2025, an increase of 22 percent.

Examples of various companies, large and small, show how the labor potential and talent of status holders can be used effectively, provided that employers take into account the requirements of integration, allowing status holders to combine work and integration. Landscaping company 'De Groene Brug' found hardworking employees in status holders in times of tight labor markets. UMC Utrecht offers an orientation program for status holders in healthcare, which prepares them for jobs in this sector. ABN AMRO's Reboot program offers status holders a new career that matches their education level and background, supported by coaching and training.

## Initiatives and support for working with newcomers

Various factors contribute to the higher labor participation of newcomers. The labor market is tight, which motivates employers to utilize the labor potential of newcomers. Initiatives such as Tent Nederland and Refugee Talent Hub bring employers and refugees together, with paid work as the goal. Working with newcomers also brings specific challenges, including language or cultural differences that need to be bridged. Employers have started various initiatives that help with this, for example via the knowledge platform [NLwerktaanwerk.nl](https://www.nlwerktaanwerk.nl/nieuwkomers) of employers' organizations VNO-NCW and MKB Nederland.

Employers who work with newcomers can claim various subsidy schemes and tax benefits. For the group of status holders, for example, there is the [Subsidy Scheme for Support for Employers](https://www.voeringvanbeleidszw.nl/subsidies-en-verzekeringen/subsidieregeling-steun-werkgevers-inzet-statushouders-sowis) (SOWIS). An overview of subsidy schemes and tax benefits can be found via the [Refugee Employment Guide](https://www.ser.nl/nl/thema/werkwijzer-vluchtelingen/werken/verzekeringen#:~:text=Gemeenten%20kunnen%20werkgevers%20die%20een,de%20loonwaarde%20van%20de%20vluchteling.) of the Social Economic Council (SER). Working with newcomers has a positive effect on the so-called Social Return on Investment (SROI). Companies that have won a tender from a government institution are given SROI objectives that they can achieve by creating job opportunities for people at a distance from the labor market, including newcomers. Meeting SROI goals can increase the likelihood of future government contracts.

The rest of this report takes a closer look at the different groups of newcomers and their experiences on the labor market. We use examples of various organizations that work with newcomers: Vebego Groen, Vermaat Catering, Landgoed Zonheuvel, landscaping company De Groene Brug, UMC Utrecht and ABN AMRO.
