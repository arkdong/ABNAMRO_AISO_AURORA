# Business services stay the course in turbulent times

[Sector forecast](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/sectorprognoses/index.html)

[Business services](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/zakelijk-diensten/index.html)

05/12/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[Mario Bersem](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/mario-bersem/index.html)

The Dutch economy shows resilience amid international uncertainty. According to ABN AMRO, the business services sector will grow by 1.5 percent in 2025, followed by 1 percent in 2026 and 1.5 percent in 2027. Although 76 percent of entrepreneurs in the sector want to stop within ten years, succession has often not yet been arranged. This leads to risks for continuity, with loss of knowledge, employment and capital.

The business services sector provides services to all other sectors and is strongly intertwined with the role of the Netherlands as an international trading country and financial center. The international tensions are partly ignoring the sector for the time being. The American import duties only apply to goods, which means that their direct effect on the sector is limited. Services exports do suffer indirectly through exposure to international goods trade.

[Read also: Business services rely heavily on the American market and technology](/nl/business/insights/sectors-and-trends/sectorprognoses/business-services/business-services-relies-heavily-on-American-market-and-technology.html)

Business services comprise three main groups: professional services (accountancy, law, consultancy), facility services (cleaning, security, catering) and HR services (employment agencies, secondment companies, payroll companies). Together these industries represent 17 percent of GDP and the sector tends to grow in line with the broader economy.

## Staff shortages are the biggest obstacle

Entrepreneurs in the business services sector are faced with major staff shortages, according to the Economic Survey of the Netherlands (COEN) of the Central Bureau of Statistics (CBS). No fewer than 45.5 percent of entrepreneurs say they experience a 'shortage of personnel' as the biggest obstacle to business operations, compared to an average of 33.3 percent across all sectors. Entrepreneurs in the employment mediation, security and cleaning sectors find it even more difficult to find staff, with percentages in the COEN of 52 percent, 66 percent and 71 percent respectively. Moreover, the number of unemployed in the third quarter of 2025 will be slightly higher than the number of vacancies for the first time in a long time, which may indicate a turnaround.

The important employment mediation sector contributes 20 percent to the added value of total business services and fulfills a crucial role in the Dutch economy by mediating talent where it is needed. Historically, the industry is highly cyclical and benefits when the economic tide is good, as it is now. But due to the long-term tightness on the labor market, mediation is faltering and the industry, which includes employment agencies, secondment agencies, payroll companies, self-employed mediators and recruitment and selection agencies, is shrinking. The new temporary employment collective labor agreement poses an additional challenge for temporary workers, due to cost increases for employers and resulting declining demand.

## Business succession in business services

A survey among 519 entrepreneurs, conducted by Ipsos I&O on behalf of ABN AMRO, shows that entrepreneurs who want to retire often do not yet have a business succession. Entrepreneurs in business services are no exception to this: 76 percent want to manage their own company for a maximum of ten years, but approximately half of them do not yet have an intended successor. 163 entrepreneurs from the business services sector were interviewed, mainly small SMEs. Three quarters have a maximum of ten employed persons and a quarter have more than ten employed persons. Self-employed persons are excluded from the sample.

Of the entrepreneurs interviewed in the broader business services sector, 75 percent are founders of the company. Only 6 percent of the entrepreneurs surveyed took over the business from family. The remaining 19 percent took over the business from someone outside the family. A similar pattern is visible in business succession plans.

When asked about their intended successor, 19 percent of entrepreneurs who want to quit within ten years point to a successor within the company and 9 percent to someone from outside. Only 13 percent point to someone in the family, which is relatively low compared to other sectors. The business services sector has a large number of partnerships, often operating within the legal form of a private limited company (B.V.). This form of collaboration, popular with lawyers, accountants and consultants, is characterized by the fact that succession takes place among 'mates' or 'partners' who are both employees and co-owners.

Of the entrepreneurs who say they want to quit within ten, 24 percent are not yet working on succession. Another 24 percent are looking but have not found anyone yet and 7 percent say they want to close the company.
