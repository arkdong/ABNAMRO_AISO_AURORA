# This is how No Waste Army shakes up the food chain

[Entrepreneurial story](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/ondernemershalen/index.html)

[Retail](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/retail/index.html)

02/09/2025 • 5 minutes reading

[Henk Hofstede](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/henk-hofstede/index.html)

What do you do when you see farmers left with tons of good food? For four entrepreneurs from Breda, the answer was simple: take action. In 2023 they founded No Waste Army. With their company – nominated for the ABN AMRO Newcomer Retail Award 2025–2026 – they are shaking up the food chain.

Growers Robin and Adriaan were struggling with a surplus of apples and pears. Due to hail damage, no one wanted to take them away, even though the taste was still perfect. Or take organic growers Hanneke and Bart. They were left with thousands of kilos of raspberries because the market opted for cheaper ones from Spain.

## Combating food waste

These are just two examples of a major problem. Farmers and growers do not lose their harvest due to failed agreements, foreign competition or because products are not 'beautiful enough'. Time for a counter-offensive, says [No Waste Army](https://nowastearmy.nl/). They pay farmers a fair price, process the saved harvest into shelf-stable products and put them in their box to prevent waste. A package full of soups, sauces, juices and other non-perishable products. More than 21,000 subscribers receive the box at home every quarter.

> “They pay farmers a fair price, process the rescued harvest into shelf-stable products and put them in their box to prevent waste.”

## From outrage to movement

No Waste Army was founded in 2023 out of pure outrage. The four founders – Thibaud van der Steen, Frank Haagen, Jacco Grinwis and Stijn Markusse, also owner of Boerschappen – could not bear to see farmers left with hundreds of millions of kilos of high-quality food. They decided to join forces.

“We want to shake up the food chain,” says Thibaud van der Steen. "The food box is the means to achieve that mission. Changing the food chain is very noble, but you need turnover, traffic and volume for that. Thanks to the commitment of our subscribers, we can make an impact and immediately pay farmers a fair price. We expect that we will grow to 23,500 subscribers and a turnover of 4.5 million euros by the end of this year."

## A good story leads to explosive growth

The approach works. In two years, No Waste Army has saved almost 1.5 million kilos of food and the number of subscribers has grown explosively. “3,000 new subscribers joined last month,” says Van der Steen. “We would never have dared to dream that in 2023.”

> “We want to shake up the food chain.”

According to him, the power lies in the stories. "We are good storytellers. People are far removed from their food these days, but we make the story of farmers and growers visible again. That is catching on. In recent weeks we have been in a media storm. From NOS to Radio 1: everyone wanted to report on our rescue operations for raspberry and potato growers. This allows us to reach an audience of millions. In addition, we are a bit lucky. This seems to be exactly the right momentum. People are becoming more aware of where their food comes from."

## Collaboration with supermarkets

The impact does not go unnoticed. Two major supermarket chains have already contacted us for a meeting. Van der Steen: "Up until now it has been just talking, but it is a great compliment that we are seen, while we are so young. We show supermarkets that you can really tackle food waste, if you just want to and put in the effort. Hopefully the day will come when they say: we are going to do this together. Because we do not want to become a supplier, but really fight together against food waste."

His dream? A new shelf in the supermarket - somewhere between fresh and long-life - full of products that prevent waste. From soups and sauces to juices and crooked vegetables.” Van der Steen: “As far as I'm concerned, the first supermarket that dares to embrace this will emerge as the winner. There is a huge opportunity for supermarkets, also in terms of marketing.”

## Only winners

Van der Steen sees No Waste Army not only as a food savior, but also as a connector. "We actually have a distribution problem in the Netherlands. On the one hand, food remains on the land, on the other hand, hundreds of thousands of people are dependent on the Food Bank. If we can link the two, everyone will emerge as a winner."

That is why No Waste Army is already setting up separate crowdfunding campaigns for food that the company cannot fit in its boxes. For example, for five euros you can donate five kilos of 'rescued' carrots to the Food Bank. In this way, 500,000 kilos of fruit and vegetables have already ended up at the Food Bank.

> “We actually have a distribution problem in the Netherlands.”

But Van der Steen sees more potential. "The next step is to set up a platform in collaboration with large companies. In an ideal world, companies donate money, we buy food from farmers for a fair price and food banks place their orders. We take care of the distribution and tell the stories of the farmers and the companies that make this possible. That makes it tangible, and that is exactly what many companies are looking for. The first steps have been taken. We have run the first pilot and one of the largest companies in our country wants to invest."

## Food waste does not stop at the national border

For now, the focus is on further growing and stabilizing the food box. “First things first,” says Van der Steen. "The foundation must be good. We are growing explosively and that has a great impact on our company. We must maintain that." There are also plans to roll out the concept abroad. "We are in an advanced stage of crossing the border. This problem does not stop at the border."

## Nomination as appreciation

The nomination for the ABN AMRO Newcomer Retail Award 2025–2026 feels like recognition to the team. "It is a token of appreciation and a boost for our team. We started two years ago with an idea on 1 A4 sheet. We have now become a movement that you can no longer ignore," Van der Steen concludes proudly.
