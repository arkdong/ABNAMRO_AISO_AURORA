# Healthcare sector also innovates without a cabinet

[Column](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/column.html)

[Healthcare](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/healthcare/index.html)

03/06/2025 • 4 minutes reading

[David Bolscher](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/authors/david-bolscher/index.html)

The fall of the cabinet is a setback for the challenges the Netherlands faces. Dossiers on housing construction and nitrogen are being delayed and a healthcare agreement with resources for digitalization has not been forthcoming for the time being. That's okay, because healthcare providers are already successfully innovating in response to the tight labor market. The next government would be better off spending additional resources on welfare and prevention.

The Schoof Cabinet's ambition to make healthcare more accessible was great. The cabinet's showpiece, halving the deductible by 2027, is now in doubt with the fall of the cabinet. The Court of Audit [recently ruled](https://www.rekenkamer.nl/publicaties/verslag/2025/05/21/vo-2024-vws) that the policy of the Ministry of Health, Welfare and Sport (VWS) is not clear or does not work. It is unclear whether the agreements in the Integrated Care Agreement (IZA) contribute to future-proof care.

In any case, digitalization and innovation contribute to future-proof healthcare. This was stated several times by Minister Fleur Agema of VWS. Healthcare providers recognize this as an important way to keep healthcare accessible. However, innovation is hampered by the complexity of the healthcare system with varying financing laws and the lack of rates for new digital forms of healthcare provision.

> “All kinds of welfare questions are asked by healthcare providers. The government must therefore invest in welfare and prevention, and encourage companies to make choices that contribute to a healthy society.”

## Healthcare providers do not go on hold

While Agema recently managed to free up a few hundred million euros for digitalization in healthcare after a lot of haggling with the Minister of Finance, healthcare providers had already implemented numerous innovations. For example, hospitals fill empty gaps in schedules with the help of AI, nursing homes operate with fewer caregivers thanks to bed sensors, and home care has to visit a client less often thanks to a tool to put on a support stocking.

The extra money that Agema wanted to make available is always welcome, but unfortunately digitalization does not solve all problems. Healthcare providers are mainly confronted with excessive demand for care, complexity of the healthcare system, and the question of which care requests they are responsible for. In this way, all kinds of welfare questions are asked by healthcare providers. The government must therefore invest in well-being and prevention, and encourage companies to make choices that contribute to a healthy society.

[Read also: Companies take the lead for a healthy society](/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/healthcare/bedrijven-aan-zet-voor-een-gezonde-samenleving.html)

Healthcare providers are already looking at health more broadly, but they cannot do this without policy. In addition, a healthcare provider is paid for providing care, and not for preventing care. In fact, the healthcare provider who prevents care loses turnover. The current government cut back on prevention and even threatened to increase taxes on sports. More people are becoming ill and the demand on scarce healthcare providers is only increasing.

> “Agema wanted to solve this problem with an extra pot of money. The question is whether this will fix the cracks in the system and help people with social health problems. The next cabinet can show courage by taking responsibility for a healthy society.”

## Pursue a broader policy on welfare and health

The next cabinet can mainly be of added value to the healthcare sector by making clear choices. A minister with a clear vision about what can be expected from healthcare, and which problems can be solved with other ministries. For example, care needs often arise from social problems that should therefore be addressed at an earlier stage, namely with sufficient housing, good education and a social safety net.

In addition, healthcare providers in mental health care, youth care and home care are helped with major adjustments to the complex healthcare system. In particular, the sale of care to different municipalities, a care office and a health insurer is time-intensive and prevents collaboration between care providers and care purchasers to innovate. Too often, a measure that reduces the need for care is not reimbursed because the savings end up with another party. For example, adjustments in homes such as a stair lift or a shower chair are reimbursed by municipalities, but the savings from fewer care hours end up at the care office.

Agema wanted to solve this problem with an extra pot of money. The question is whether this will mend the cracks in the system and help people with social health problems. The next government can show courage by taking responsibility for a healthy society. This requires investments in welfare and prevention, agreements with the business community and central purchasing of mental health care, youth care and social support. Leave the innovation for accessible care to healthcare providers.
