### Put the reader centre stage
## **Put the reader centre stage** 

All information must be correct and clear, and must not be misleading. Specific rules apply to sustainability claims to prevent greenwashing. Sustainability claims are all expressions relating to the sustainability of products, services or business operations in terms of their environmental, social or governance (ESG) aspects. These claims must be accurate, representative, specific, substantiated and up to date. In addition, the information must be understandable, appropriate and easy to find. To align with this, we follow the AFM’s Guidelines on Sustainability Claims. See Ruby (Confluence) for more information and examples (in Dutch). 

Don’t write about internal processes or products, as that will not interest your readers. Put yourself in your readers’ shoes and anticipate their questions. Why is this message useful for _them_ ?

**Wording your message** 

Question-and-answer constructions are often used in Dutch but are less common in English and should be avoided, as they can sound unnatural. 

‘If you suddenly need money, you can overdraw your Privélimiet Plus account.’ 

‘Your washing machine always seems to break at the worst time – when you’ve just been on an expensive holiday, for example. Our Privélimiet Plus account lets you overdraw, so you can replace your washing machine right away.’

### Avoid figurative language
## **Avoid figurative language** 

Sayings and proverbs are colourful, but they’re not precise. Some people can’t read very well or may not be native speakers of English. They probably won’t know the meaning of certain expressions, so the message will be lost on them. That is why it’s better to avoid figurative language. 

The section on ‘Inclusive writing’ in chapter 6 explains how to make sure your text includes everyone. 

‘Our employees will be happy to explain the most appropriate mortgage in your situation.’ 

‘ABN AMRO reports to the Dutch Authority for the Financial Markets (AFM).’

### 1.2 Define your objective: why are you writing this text?
## **1.2 Define your objective: why are you writing this text?** 

The information you provide must always enable your readers to take the next step. But what exactly do they need to do when they have read your text? Formulate your objectives in terms of the behaviour you want to elicit. 

1. Why does ABN AMRO insist on receiving my annual results in digital format? 

2. What are the advantages (and disadvantages) for me? 

3. What if I can only submit a hard-copy version of my annual results? 

4. Can my accountant submit my annual results in digital format? 

5. Where can I find more information about this topic?

### Advertising model under pressure due to new European legislation  (2022-06-28, sector: Analyse)
Although it is not yet possible to determine exactly how many companies in the Netherlands will be affected, it is already clear that many websites depend on advertising revenue. Of the ten million most-visited websites worldwide, almost [47 per cent](https://w3techs.com/technologies/history_overview/advertising/all "Historical trends in the usage statistics of advertising networks for websites") use an ‘ad network’. This is a technological solution that aggregates online ad space from publishers and offers it to advertisers. The extent to which these websites will still be able to generate revenue through personalised advertising is likely to decline sharply. The new process requirements make it easier for internet users to refuse tracking. The consequences of redesigning such a process should not be underestimated.
