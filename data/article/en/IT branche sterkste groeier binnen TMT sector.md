The Dutch economy continues to grow strongly and the TMT sector is benefiting. It is mainly IT companies that are showing strong growth, and TV content companies are also on the up. However, the sector shows major differences, and branches such as publishers and printers are struggling.

## Favourable economic climate

The economic climate is favourable for the TMT sector. The Dutch economy grew by 3.2% in 2017, and in 2018 GDP will increase by 2.9%. The main economic indicators are also positive. Producer confidence and consumer confidence are high, capacity utilisation in industry is high, exports continue to pick up and the number of jobs and vacancies keeps rising.

## Fewer and fewer obstacles in the sector, but labour shortages remain a problem

Entrepreneurs in the sector experience fewer obstacles to running their businesses. However, the labour shortage remains a major problem. More than one-fifth of entrepreneurs are severely affected by this. Business confidence in the sector has fallen slightly, but at 13.3 it is still at a high level.

## IT sector is the driving force; publishers, printers and telecoms in the red

The IT companies segment, the largest within TMT, continues to grow strongly. Ongoing digitalisation and a favourable economic climate are the reasons for this. Wholesale trade in ICT and TV content companies also benefit from the economic boom. However, there are also segments that are struggling. Publishers, printers and telecoms will also show revenue decline next year.
