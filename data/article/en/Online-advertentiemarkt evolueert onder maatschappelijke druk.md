# Online advertising market evolves under social pressure

[Mario Bersem](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/auteurs/mario-bersem/index.html)

Changing social standards are putting several principles of the online advertising market under pressure. Full transparency and control over the use of personal data are becoming the new standard. Contextual advertising will gain ground.

Google knows someone’s interests based on their search behaviour. Social platform Instagram has insight into users through likes, posts and followed accounts. These data allow targeted adverts.

Most other websites, unlike such ‘walled gardens’, cannot collect so many insights about their users on their own. That is why personalised adverts at smaller publishers often rely on third-party data, including browsing behaviour on previously visited websites. The use of such tracking data will soon become harder.

## Small publishers under pressure

For many publishers and advertisers, the big platform companies will continue to play an important role. The reach and amount of available visitor data at small publishers are not enough to interest advertisers one-to-one. Conversely, small advertisers want a simple way to reach their target group online. So both parties will continue to lean on platforms that bring supply and demand for advertising space together. Google is the market leader here.

## Transparency as a precondition

Besides the power of big technology companies, the consumer’s will also determines where the online advertising market will go. Their privacy awareness will likely strengthen in the coming years through stricter enforcement of the European privacy law GDPR and the requirements of the Digital Services Act. The latter requires clear communication about data use for online adverts and makes it easier to say “no” to tracking. Both requirements put power — at least in part — back in the hands of the internet user.
