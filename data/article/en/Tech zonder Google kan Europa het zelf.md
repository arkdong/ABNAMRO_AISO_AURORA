# Tech without Google: can Europe do it itself?

[Podcast](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/in-gesprek/podcast/index.html)

[Technology, Media & Telecom](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/technologie/index.html)

16/10/2025 • 2 minutes read

[Julia Krauwer](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/auteurs/julia-krauwer/index.html)

Without US cloud services and chips from Taiwan, much of our digital life would grind to a halt. Yet Europe still relies heavily on technology from elsewhere. How did this happen, and what should Europe do to stand more firmly on its own feet digitally? In this episode of the podcast Achter de Cijfers, journalist Esther van Rijswijk and sector banker Julia Krauwer explore the challenges and opportunities of digital sovereignty.

For decades, Europe trusted American technology. But the re-election of Trump and his unpredictable policy exposed how vulnerable that dependency makes us. What if American tech companies stopped providing services to Europe? The spectre of governments, hospitals and financial transactions grinding to a halt adds urgency to developing a plan B.

## Europe’s path to technological sovereignty

## Having sufficient influence

According to Julia Krauwer, sector banker Technology, Media and Telecom, digital sovereignty does not mean Europe has to do everything itself. It means we must have influence where it matters. Whether it is cloud infrastructure, artificial intelligence or chip technology, Europe should invest strategically and work together to build a stronger position.

Europe also has the chance to develop technology that fits its own values: transparent, fair and focused on the public interest. Think of AI that does not discriminate, social media that are not purely geared towards addiction, and cloud solutions that safeguard privacy and security.
