## Page 4: Agenda

Formats are structured templates or guidelines that determine how content is presented. They are essential for publishing news articles on ABN AMRO Insights. They create consistency and recognisability, which improves readability for readers.

## Page 5: Basic components

Every format uses a number of standard building blocks. These include the opening photo or header image, title, introduction, subheadings, tags and related articles. A chart, quote, PDF or additional images are not standard building blocks, but they are valuable rich-content additions.

1. Opening photo or header image: choose an image that strengthens the theme, and preferably the title.
2. Heading or title: choose a heading that speaks to the reader. Maximum 56 characters including spaces.
3. Labels: state the format or content type and the primary sector for the article.
4. Publication date and author(s): the publication date and author names must be entered manually and are shown for every article, including entrepreneur stories. On the overview page, however, only one author is shown.
5. Introduction: maximum 50 words or 300 characters including spaces. The first sentence always contains the core of the publication. Do not include a URL in the intro text.
6. Subheadings: place a subheading after roughly every third paragraph. Subheadings are also relevant for SEO as h2 headings and are strongly recommended. Sub-subheadings (h3) are less relevant and less recommended, but they can help readability in extremely long texts.
7. Primary and secondary sectors and themes: enter the primary sector in the template. This is mandatory, except optionally for cross-sector publications. Add secondary sectors and themes when needed.
8. Read also: articles from the same primary sector are loaded automatically.

## Page 6: Rich Content - 10 essential benefits

Rich web content refers to content that is visually appealing, interactive and informative. It is good for search engines and for the reader. Examples include images, infographics, video and audio clips, quotes, PDF downloads, internal links and interactive tools such as Local Focus charts.

1. Tell an attractive story: multimedia elements can help tell a more engaging story, so the message is communicated more effectively and remembered better.
2. Better user experience: rich web content makes websites more attractive and dynamic, creating a more positive user experience.
3. Higher engagement: multimedia elements can keep visitors on the website longer and make them engage more actively with the content.
4. Better information transfer: complex ideas are often easier to communicate through visual aids such as videos and Local Focus charts.
5. Sharing on social media: visually attractive content is shared more often on social platforms, which can increase reach and traffic to the website.
6. Higher conversion rates: attractive and interactive content can persuade users to take action, such as clicking through to other articles or product and service pages.
7. Better SEO performance: search engines value rich content, especially when it is well optimised, which can improve ranking in search results.
8. Better brand perception: professional and attractive content can improve the perception of ABN AMRO, making it look more reliable and innovative.
9. Accessibility for different learning styles: people learn in different ways. Rich content can support visual, auditory and kinaesthetic learning styles.
10. More options for personalisation: interactive elements can give users a personalised experience, which can increase satisfaction and loyalty.

## Page 9: Report

A report goes beyond an analysis because it gives the reader unique facts, figures and colour, and expresses a clear point of view. A report contains both qualitative and quantitative analysis of one or more trends and developments. It is supported with several charts and sources. Short interviews and quotes from entrepreneurs and experts support the research and add colour to sometimes dry figures.

1. Opening photo or header image: choose an image that supports the subject, and preferably the title.
2. Heading or title: choose a heading that speaks to the reader. Maximum 56 characters including spaces.
3. Publication date and author(s): the publication date and author names must be entered manually and are shown for every article, including entrepreneur stories. On the overview page only one author is shown.
4. Introduction: maximum 50 words or 300 characters including spaces. The first sentence always contains the core of the publication. Do not include a URL in the intro text.
5. Subheadings: place a subheading after roughly every third paragraph. Subheadings are also relevant for SEO as h2 headings and are strongly recommended. Sub-subheadings (h3) are less relevant and less recommended, but they can help readability in extremely long texts.

Characteristic elements: PDF report, Local Focus charts and one quote.

## Page 12: Entrepreneur story

An entrepreneur story is an interview or personal story from an entrepreneur. Choose a theme the entrepreneur has experience with, such as bankruptcy, growth, coaching, financing or business acquisition, and from which the reader can learn.

1. Opening photo or header image: always use a landscape photo of the entrepreneur or company. If that is not possible, use a photo of the company.
2. Heading or title: preferably include the name of the entrepreneur or company, or create a strong title without a quote. If the name is too long, include it in the introduction. The reader must immediately know who is in the photo and who they are dealing with. Maximum 56 characters including spaces.
3. Publication date and author(s): the publication date and author names must be entered manually and are shown for every article, including entrepreneur stories. On the overview page only one author is shown.
4. Introduction: maximum 50 words or 300 characters including spaces. Use the standard intro requirements, but add the company name or entrepreneur name unless it is already in the heading.

Characteristic element: recognisable landscape photo of the entrepreneur or company.

## Page 13: Interview with an ABN AMRO colleague

An interview with one of ABN AMRO's own sector economists, bankers, managers or spokespeople. An interview on Insights works best when it discusses a current reason or one theme where the conversational form adds value compared with a report, analysis or other article. The added value can also simply be variation in formats.

1. Opening photo or header image: always use a landscape photo of the ABN AMRO colleague. If that is not possible, use ABN AMRO imagery.
2. Heading or title: preferably include the name of the ABN AMRO interviewee followed by a sharp quote. If the name is too long, use only the quote and put the name in the introduction. The reader must immediately know who is in the photo and who they are dealing with.
3. Publication date and author(s): the publication date and author names must be entered manually and are shown for every article, including entrepreneur stories. On the overview page only one author is shown.
4. Introduction: maximum 50 words or 300 characters including spaces. Use the standard intro requirements, but add the name of the ABN AMRO colleague and, where possible, the reason for the interview.

Characteristic element: ABN AMRO colleague portrait or image.

## Page 26: OMO project - Bankwide European Accessibility Act project

The European Accessibility Act (EAA) is an EU directive that aims to improve the accessibility of products and services. The EAA defines minimum accessibility standards.

- Accessibility: A11Y, an abbreviation for accessibility.
- Scope: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).
- Tool: WCAG Checklist from Cardan.
- Objective: comply with the EAA. As a bank, we also feel responsible for making our products, services and information accessible to potential customers and to customers with hearing or visual impairments.
- Deadline: 28 June 2025.

Why:

1. Accessibility legislation for abnamro.nl and the English website.
2. New Brand Experience.
3. Content quality.
4. Reducing the size of the website.

What and when:

1. Content optimisation.
2. Start in June 2024.
3. Deadline in June 2025.

Who and how:

1. CoE Content Design.
2. EC&E.
3. Content optimisation cleanup process.

## Page 27: OMO Deluxe checklist (WCAG)

Accessibility:

- Do not create subheadings by making text bold.
- Set up all copy as simply as possible at B1 level.
- Keep the page text as short as possible.
- Make all link and CTA texts accessible, with additional explanatory text in Tridion between square brackets if needed.
- Follow all copy guidelines and A11Y guidelines.

Assets also need A11Y:

- Use all assets, including images, icons and PDFs, from the DAM.
- Give every asset correct alt text, or no alt text when it is decorative.
- Make all visual information available in text on the page.
- Provide subtitles for all videos.

Quality:

- No spelling errors or broken links.
- Page metadata: review date updated, content administrator filled, content properties completed, index/no-index set, and EN/NL URL filled.
- Use the correct components.

BX and level:

- Rewrite u to je.
- Rewrite according to BX tone guidelines.
- Rewrite to B1 level.
- Rewrite toward BX guidelines.

## Page 34: Photography do's and don'ts

Do:

1. High quality: deliver the largest possible file size, at least 200 KB, for a sharp and professional look.
2. Inclusivity: use diverse images that realistically represent Dutch society. Avoid stereotypes.
3. Recognisable and real situations: choose real, recognisable environments that communicate the right atmosphere and sector. Put people in the spotlight where possible, because that makes communication personal and human.
4. Natural colours: use natural lighting and warm colours. Add a subtle ABN AMRO green accent.

Do not:

1. No 3D or graphic elements: use only real screens from devices. Avoid holograms, symbols, text and floating screens.
2. Avoid theatrical emotions: do not choose images with exaggerated emotions or wooden blocks to represent complex concepts or symbolic themes. Prefer a general atmospheric image that reflects the sector or theme.
3. No overexposed photos: ensure balanced lighting. Avoid heavy contrasts, sun flares and black-and-white images. Do not use shots at sunrise or sunset.

## Page 35: Legal photo rights

Pay attention to usage rights when requesting or passing on images. This also applies to external parties that create images. Whether the image is purchased from Shutterstock or supplied by third parties, the same usage-rights rules apply to every image.

The following information is stored in the DAM, the Digital Asset Management system:

- Supplier.
- Licence(s).
- End date for usage rights.

Standard usage-rights agreements by category:

1. Shutterstock: Digital Owned Media licence. Photos marked editorial use only may not be purchased.
2. Author portraits by photographer Hannie Verhoeven: indefinite period for Digital Owned Media, Social Media and Internal licences.
3. Entrepreneur stories and customer-supplied images: indefinite period for Digital Owned Media, Social Media and Internal licence.
4. External media agencies, photographers or designers: pass on information about bought-out usage rights using these four questions:
   - Who supplied or created the image?
   - Which licence has been bought and what may ABN AMRO use the image for: Digital Owned Media, Social Media or Internal?
   - For how long does ABN AMRO have the licence(s) for image rights?
   - Are there other conditions?

## Page 36: Workflow for delivering photos

Option 1: Search in the DAM first.

- Start your search in the DAM. A useful tip is to use English search terms, just as you would in Shutterstock.
- Searching in the DAM can be difficult and there is still a lot of old image material that is not fully aligned with the new BIS. When in doubt, consult a Content Expert.
- Explore the managed folders or saved selections by sector or category. These selections are carefully maintained by a Visual Designer and meet BIS standards.

Option 2: New Shutterstock photo.

- If there is no suitable image in the DAM, a photo can be purchased via Shutterstock. The correct licence is "Media Digital License".
- Photos with editorial use only may not be purchased.
- The image must meet the BIS photography guidelines. If it does not meet these guidelines, it cannot be uploaded to the DAM. When in doubt, have it checked before buying and delivering it.

Option 3: Image supplied by an external person or external design agency.

- Provide a clear briefing on quality requirements and guidelines before an external party delivers visual material. Send the correct guidelines and PDF guideline documents from the start to ensure proper content development.
- If a customer supplies visual material, for example for entrepreneur stories, it must be communicated that usage rights are automatically given to ABN AMRO. This means ABN AMRO may use the image for communication about the customer or subject, for an indefinite period, and on all online, internal and social media channels.
