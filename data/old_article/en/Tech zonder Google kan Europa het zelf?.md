Without American cloud services and chips from Taiwan, a large part of our digital lives would grind to a halt. Even so, Europe relies heavily on technology from elsewhere. How did that happen, and what does Europe need to do to stand more firmly on its own digital feet? In this episode of the Achter de Cijfers podcast, journalist Esther van Rijswijk and sector banker Julia Krauwer dive into the challenges and opportunities of digital sovereignty.

For decades, Europe relied on American technology. But Trump's re-election and his unpredictable policies exposed how vulnerable that dependence makes us. What if American tech companies stopped supplying services to Europe? The nightmare scenario of governments, hospitals and financial transactions grinding to a halt is fuelling the urgency of developing a plan B.

## Having enough influence

According to Julia Krauwer, Sector Banker Technology, Media & Telecom, digital sovereignty does not mean Europe has to do everything itself, but that we must have influence on the crucial points. Whether it concerns cloud infrastructure, artificial intelligence or chip technology, Europe has to invest strategically and work together in order to build a stronger position.

Europe also now has the opportunity to develop technology that aligns with its own values: transparent, fair and focused on the public interest. Think of AI that does not discriminate, social media that is not aimed purely at addiction, and cloud solutions that safeguard privacy and security.
