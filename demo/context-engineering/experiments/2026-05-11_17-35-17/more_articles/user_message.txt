## **When do we use capital letters? ABN AMRO’s standards are listed below:**

- **Proper nouns:** when proper nouns are capitalised in English, only significant words 

   - in the heading are capitalised (not short words such as ‘of’ and ‘the’). Examples are the University of Amsterdam, the Secretary of State.

   - **Department names:** always capitalise, for example Group Compliance, Group Sustainable Development, Press Office. However, when referring to the business area or market segment, no capitals are needed, as in: ‘Group Sustainable Development concentrates on **sustainable development** .’

   - **Government and politics:** organisations, ministries, departments, treaties, acts, etc., generally take capital letters when their full name is used. For example: Ministry of Finance, Health and Safety at Work Act. Do not capitalise descriptions: the French **parliament** , the American , etc. The full names of political **finance department**

### Advertising model under pressure due to new European legislation  (2022-06-28, sector: Analyse)
Although it is not yet possible to determine exactly how many companies in the Netherlands will be affected, it is already clear that many websites depend on advertising revenue. Of the ten million most-visited websites worldwide, almost [47 per cent](https://w3techs.com/technologies/history_overview/advertising/all "Historical trends in the usage statistics of advertising networks for websites") use an ‘ad network’. This is a technological solution that aggregates online ad space from publishers and offers it to advertisers. The extent to which these websites will still be able to generate revenue through personalised advertising is likely to decline sharply. The new process requirements make it easier for internet users to refuse tracking. The consequences of redesigning such a process should not be underestimated.

### Privacy protection puts online advertising at a turning point  (2022-12-15, sector: Rapport)
In 2021, people spent on average about [seven hours](https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2022-october-global-statshot "DIGITAL 2022: OCTOBER GLOBAL STATSHOT REPORT") a day in online channels. Advertisers therefore spend billions a year to show their message to website visitors and app users. This market is growing fast; in 2021 this amount in Europe had risen to [92 billion euros](https://iabeurope.eu/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/adex-2021-advertising-report.pdf "DIGITAL ADVERTISING IN EUROPE") – an increase of 30.5 per cent compared with the previous year. In the Netherlands, digital ad spend exceeded advertising spend in traditional media for the first time in 2016 and by now the digital ad spend is already more than twice as large. For the owners of websites, apps and other content – also known as ‘publishers’ – the internet offers opportunities to monetise their ad space. For example, almost half of the largest websites use a so-called ad network. This is a technological solution that collects online ad space from publishers and offers it to advertisers. Besides advertisers and publishers, there are numerous intermediaries and other service providers active in the online advertising market. Advertisers are supported by media, marketing and advertising agencies, which help determine the most effective channels for online ad campaigns and often also play a role in the associated purchase of ad space. There are also numerous so-called AdTech players, which use technological solutions to facilitate the buying and selling of online ad space. Personalised advertising as standard practice.

### Digital media grow through transformation and new channels  (2024-08-22, sector: Analyse)
Digital ad spend drives media sector. Developments in the advertising market are a good barometer for developments within the media sector. In 2023, around € 5.2 billion was spent on advertising in the Netherlands, of which 71 per cent was digital advertising, that is € 3.7 billion, according to the VIA/Deloitte Digital Advertising Spend report for 2023 (see chart). Revenue growth for digital advertising was 5 per cent compared with 2022. Compared with an average annual growth rate of 11 per cent in the ten years before, this was relatively low. In 2024, ABN AMRO expects 9 per cent revenue growth for digital advertising, more in line with the historical average. Growth rates of 11 per cent or more from the past are no longer to be expected because digitalisation has now progressed further and some submarkets are saturated. Paid search, for example, is growing somewhat more slowly, while online video, social media and digital audio are growing somewhat faster.
