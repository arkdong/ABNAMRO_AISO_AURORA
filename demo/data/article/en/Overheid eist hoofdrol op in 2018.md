# Government claims lead role in 2018

[Technology, Media & Telecom](https://www.abnamro.nl/nl/zakelijk/insights/sectoren-en-trends/technologie/index.html)

Technology, Media and Telecom (TMT) is the sector where the new economy becomes visible first. It is the sector that facilitates the digitalisation of the rest of the economy and benefits from it.

It is also the sector where digital disruption has hit fastest and hardest. Regulation often lags behind, but this year governments and regulators are catching up.

## Large revenue differences within the TMT sector in 2018 and 2019

The IT sector benefits from economic growth and the outlook for 2018 is positive. ABN AMRO expects the revenue of IT companies to keep growing in 2018 (+6 per cent) and in 2019 (+5 per cent). At the same time, there are large differences with other TMT sectors. In telecoms and among publishers and printers there is prolonged decline. In telecoms, revenue will probably fall further: by 1.5 per cent in 2018 and by 2 per cent in 2019. Publishers’ revenue will also decrease: by 3 per cent in 2018 and by 4 per cent in 2019. For printing companies, revenue will fall by 3 per cent this year and by 3.5 per cent next year.

## Regulation in the IT sector is increasing

Despite the expected growth, the sector also faces challenges, especially around privacy. Digitalisation increases the need for regulation and government oversight. As a result of the General Data Protection Regulation, companies must take additional measures. Documenting the processing of personal data and communicating about data breaches now also fall under the law. On the other hand, there is the new Intelligence and Security Services Act, which actually increases the possibilities to investigate citizens’ data.

## Government and regulators tighten their grip on the TMT sector

Although the TMT sector facilitates digitalisation in other economic sectors, it struggles to manage its own growth. "The speed at which technological developments follow one another has increased sharply in recent years," says Kasper Buiting, Sector Economist Technology, Media and Telecom at ABN AMRO. “This led to the emergence and huge growth of companies such as Google and Facebook. What we now see is that regulation often still lags behind the sector’s technological clout. This year, governments and regulators are catching up. "This applies not only to data traffic and privacy, but also to consolidation. If we look at the telecoms market, for example, we see a sector that tends to consolidate further and further, while the government wants to preserve competition and market forces. That is why we expect government oversight and increasing regulation on competition and data to set the tone across the entire TMT sector in 2018."

## Key conclusions

- Revenue differences within the TMT sector are increasing
- Government regulation in the IT sector is increasing in 2018
- Stricter oversight of competitive relations and data traffic.
